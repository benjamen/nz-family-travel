# NZ Family Travel — Content Strategy

**Generated**: 2026-05-29
**Site**: https://nzfamilytravel.co.nz

---

## Situation

The site has solid destination coverage (19 NZ destinations, 18 itineraries, 12 travel-tip pages, 10 overseas guides, weather/school holidays tools). Key gaps are:

- No age-specific planning content beyond the toddler filter page
- No skiing/winter holiday content (Queenstown, Methven, Ohakune)
- No "first trip to NZ" or "NZ for international families" guide
- No camping content (separate from holiday parks)
- Missing several destination guides the city data includes but no article covers
- No shareable "bucket list" or original-data content

---

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Age-Specific Travel Guides
**Why**: "NZ with [age group]" queries are high-intent, low-competition, and have no good NZ-specific answer. A parent planning a trip with a 10-year-old has different needs than one with a toddler. These pages rank across long-tail clusters.

### Pillar 2: Season & Type Holidays
**Why**: "best time to visit NZ with kids" exists, but there's nothing on seasonal trip types: skiing holidays, beach holidays, camping trips. These are high-volume consideration-stage searches.

### Pillar 3: Trip Planning Deep Dives
**Why**: The budget calculator, driving times tool, and school holidays calendar are strong. Written content that goes deeper — "our complete NZ road trip planning guide" — builds topical authority and links those tools together.

### Pillar 4: Honest Cost Content
**Why**: "how much does X cost" is the most-asked family travel question. The travel costs page is a start. Per-destination cost breakdowns and comparison content (NZ vs overseas alternatives) answer real purchase-stage questions.

### Pillar 5: Shareable / Original
**Why**: The overseas guides create a natural comparison surface. Original data posts ("we priced 10 NZ family holidays for 2026") get backlinks and AI citations.

## Content Format Standards

- Quick-answer box: direct 40–60 word answer at top
- Age/budget callout boxes where relevant (e.g., "For families with toddlers: …")
- Real 2026 price references (not ranges — actual prices)
- Internal links: destination → activity → itinerary → tool → cost page
- FAQPage schema on every guide
- CTA: school holiday calendar, itinerary builder, or campervan comparison
- Pinterest-optimised titles (family travel niche skews heavily Pinterest)
