## Key Takeaways

1. **The practical planning gap is wide open** — newzealand.com dominates inspiration but ignores costs, school holidays, and planning tools. Our niche is "what does this trip actually cost and when should we go?"

2. **Named author is our weakest E-E-A-T signal** — Guest New Zealand has a named author; we don't. Adding a persona (e.g., "the NZ Family Travel team" with bios) would improve AI citation likelihood.

3. **School holidays and weather pages are unique assets** — No competitor has these. They're low-competition, high-intent pages that build a moat.

4. **Campervan comparison content** — No competitor covers this with commercial depth. This is our highest-value affiliate content area.

5. **International visitors vs. domestic families** — newzealand.com targets international tourists; we should own NZ families planning domestic trips (different intent, different keywords).

## Gaps and Opportunities

- **Named author** — Add a byline/bio to all guides. Even "Written by the NZ Family Travel team" with a brief bio page lifts E-E-A-T.
- **"Is NZ good for families?" type queries** — International families researching NZ trips aren't served by our domestic-focused content. A bridge article capturing international intent could earn external links.
- **PDF/printable packing lists** — Guest NZ uses PDFs as assets. A printable NZ family packing list could earn links and build an email list.
- **School holiday deals** — Combining our school holiday calendar with accommodation deals is a high-converting content format no competitor uses.
- **"Rainy day activities NZ" by city** — Underserved, high-intent family query. We have the activities data to build this.
