## Positioning & Messaging

**Primary value proposition**: Official NZ tourism destination site. Authoritative, comprehensive, globally promoted.

**Target audience**: International visitors to NZ. Family travellers are a segment, not the primary focus.

**Positioning angle**: Destination brand ("100% Pure NZ"). Inspiration-first, not practical-planning.

**Key messaging themes**:
- Official NZ brand voice
- Inspirational/aspirational photography
- Cover every travel type (adventure, culture, family, luxury)

## Product & Features (Relevant to Family Travel)

- Destination guides (regions, cities)

### Content depth for family travel
Low. Family content is present but not deep — no cost breakdowns, no age-suitability ratings, no school holiday planning, no campervan comparisons, no specific activity reviews.

### Differentiators vs NZ Family Travel
- Massively authoritative (government + international media links)
- Official brand — media quotes from "newzealand.com" constantly
- Beautiful photography

## Pricing / Monetization

- No affiliate revenue — government-funded
- Accommodation/activity links go to partners, no commission (no commercial incentive to optimise)

---

## SEO & Content Strategy

**Organic strength**: Extreme. Thousands of NZ travel keywords dominated by or heavily influenced by newzealand.com. Impossible to outrank for generic NZ travel terms.

**Strategy**: Inspire internationally. Drive top-of-funnel awareness for NZ as destination. Doesn't serve bottom-of-funnel practical planning (costs, school holidays, rainy day activities, campervan comparisons).

---

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths
- Untouchable domain authority
- Official government backing
- Global marketing budget
- Cited by every NZ travel article worldwide

### Weaknesses
- Not a planning tool — no budget calculators, no school holiday calendar, no "rainy day activities" by city
- No affiliate integration (they have no incentive to optimise conversions)
- Family content is shallow — a section, not a specialisation
- No comparison content (campervans, accommodation types)
- No cost data (how much does it actually cost?)
- Targets international visitors, not NZ families planning a domestic trip

---

## Competitive Implications for NZ Family Travel

**Where they're strong vs. us**: DA, brand recognition, photography quality. Impossible to compete head-on for generic NZ travel queries.

**Where we're strong vs. them**: Practical depth — costs, school holidays, campervan comparisons, rainy-day options, specific activity prices, domestic NZ family angle.

**Opportunities**:
- Own the "NZ family travel planning" niche they don't serve — practical, cost-focused, domestic
- Target queries newzealand.com ignores: "cheapest NZ family holiday", "NZ school holidays 2026", "best campervans NZ families", "NZ family trip cost calculator"
- AI Overviews: our structured data + specific answers will get cited for planning queries; newzealand.com's inspirational content won't

**Threats**:
- If Tourism NZ invests in a practical planning tool or family-specific hub, their authority makes it instantly competitive
