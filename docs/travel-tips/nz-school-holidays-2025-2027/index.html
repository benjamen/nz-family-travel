NZ School Holiday Dates 2025–2027 — Planning Guide

New Zealand has four mandatory school holiday blocks per year, plus regional variations and teacher-only days that differ by region. This guide covers exact dates for 2025–2027 broken down by region, Easter variations, and which days teachers are working but students aren't. Use this to plan your family trips, book accommodation, and understand why Zealandia night tours (Wellington) and ski lifts (Queenstown) book out months ahead.

Public Holidays That Affect Travel in 2026

Public holidays change what's open, push up accommodation demand, and create long weekends families travel on. The big ones to plan around in 2026:

  • Waitangi Day — Fri 6 Feb 2026 (long weekend, beaches busy)
  • Good Friday — Fri 3 Apr 2026 (restricted trading; most shops shut, supermarkets closed)
  • Easter Sunday — Sun 5 Apr 2026 (restricted trading; falls inside autumn school holidays)
  • Easter Monday — Mon 6 Apr 2026 (shops trade normally)
  • Anzac Day — Sat 25 Apr 2026 (restricted trading until 1pm; near end of autumn break)
  • King's Birthday — Mon 1 Jun 2026 (long weekend, pre-ski-season getaways)
  • Matariki — Fri 10 Jul 2026 (long weekend, falls inside winter school holidays)
  • Labour Day — Mon 26 Oct 2026 (long weekend, end of spring season)
  • Christmas Day & Boxing Day — Fri 25 / Sat 26 Dec 2026 (restricted trading; peak summer crowds begin)

Plan Each 2026 Holiday — Activity Guides & Booking Windows

Each NZ school holiday has its own character, weather, and booking rhythm. Here's how to plan each one, with links to our detailed activity guides for every period.

Winter (July) — peak ski season. July is the busiest, most expensive holiday for snow travel. Queenstown, Wanaka, and Ruapehu fill first; book accommodation 3–4 months ahead (April for July travel) and buy ski lift passes before you arrive. The Skyline luge in Queenstown can hit 45-minute waits mid-morning — go at 9am opening or after 3pm. Wellington's Zealandia night tours (only three per night) sell out 6+ weeks ahead. See our full NZ winter school holidays activities guide, plus city-specific picks for Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch winter 2026.

Summer (December–January) — the big one. Six weeks long and overlapping Christmas and New Year. Coastal destinations book out 8–12 weeks ahead; the Coromandel and Bay of Islands fill 3+ months in advance. Campervans sell out by September and Top 10 holiday parks by August. Expect premium pricing — accommodation can be 2–3× normal cost over Boxing Day to early January. Start planning now with our summer school holidays 2026/27 guide.

Autumn (April) — best value, settled weather. Mild 15–20°C days, golden light, and far fewer crowds. Hawke's Bay, Rotorua, and the wine regions shine. Accommodation books 4–6 weeks ahead rather than months. Easter falls inside this break in 2026 — see our NZ Easter holiday ideas and Easter 2027 guide.

Spring (September–October) — the underrated one. Warming up (12–18°C), thinning crowds, and lower prices. Abel Tasman, Nelson, and the top of the South Island are ideal. Book 4–6 weeks ahead for the best value-to-experience ratio of the year.

Key Dates to Remember When Booking

Use these rules to book family travel at the right time:
  • Book accommodation 2–3 months ahead for summer (Dec–Jan) and July (ski season)
  • Book accommodation 4–6 weeks ahead for autumn (April) and spring (October)
  • Book experiences (Zealandia night tours, Te Papa programmes, ski lift passes) even earlier — some require 6–8 weeks' notice
  • Popular attractions offer school holiday programme bookings online 6+ weeks ahead — check websites when the term dates are announced
  • Zealandia (Wellington), Te Papa (Wellington), and ski lift passes should be booked immediately when dates are confirmed
  • Summer holiday parks book out Christmas–New Year; book by September for December travel
  • July = ski season; every activity involving snow or altitude is busiest during school holidays
  • Easter dates move annually — check school calendars, as some regions may close for Easter even outside formal holidays

Regional Variations (Why Dates Differ)

New Zealand schools are divided into five regional areas, each with slightly different holiday dates. This is managed by the Ministry of Education to spread holidays evenly and reduce strain on attractions during peak periods. The regions are: - **Region 1:** Auckland, Waikato (including Hamilton), Bay of Plenty (Tauranga), Rotorua, Taupo - **Region 2:** Wellington, Manawatu, Wairarapa, Hawke's Bay - **Region 3:** Taranaki, Nelson, Marlborough - **Region 4:** West Coast, Canterbury (including Christchurch) - **Region 5:** Otago (Dunedin, Queenstown), Southland (Invercargill) Region 1 and Region 2 typically differ by 1–2 weeks. Region 4 (Christchurch, Canterbury) often starts summer holidays earlier (late November vs early December) to extend their break. Always check your child's specific school website for exact dates.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do all NZ schools have the same holiday dates?
Almost — NZ divides schools into 5 regional areas with slightly staggered dates. Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch differ by 1–2 weeks. All regions share the same four holiday blocks (April, July, October, Dec–Jan), but start/end dates vary. Always check your child's school website for exact dates.
What is the best time to visit NZ with school-age kids?
Autumn (April school holidays) or spring (October school holidays) offer the best balance — fewer crowds, good weather, and moderate pricing. July has great skiing but is busiest and most expensive. Summer (Dec–Jan) is beach season but extremely crowded and expensive. If visiting from overseas, autumn and spring are most family-friendly.
When is Easter in 2026 and 2027?
Easter 2026: April 5 (falls within school holidays in most regions). Easter 2027: March 29 (falls during term time for most regions — some schools may close). Easter is determined by the lunar calendar and moves every year.
How far ahead should I book activities during school holidays?
For July (ski season) and Dec–Jan (summer): book 2–3 months ahead and popular experiences 6–8 weeks ahead. For April and October: book 4–6 weeks ahead. Zealandia night tours (Wellington) need 6+ weeks' notice. Ski lift passes should be booked before you arrive in July.
Are teacher-only days the same as school holidays?
No — teacher-only days are when teachers work but students don't attend (5–8 per year). School holidays are when the whole school closes. Teachers use these days for professional development and planning. Teacher-only days vary by region and sometimes by individual school.
Can I find the exact dates for my child's school?
Yes — check your school's website directly, or contact them by email. You can also check the Ministry of Education website (https://www.education.govt.nz) for regional calendar information. Individual schools sometimes add extra teacher-only days or term breaks.
