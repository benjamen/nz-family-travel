3-Day North Island Family Itinerary — Auckland to Rotorua

Three days is tight but doable for a North Island taste. This itinerary gets you from Auckland to Rotorua (the highlight for most kids) and back, hitting geysers, hot pools, and Māori culture without exhausting the family. Best for families with school holidays or a long weekend.

Day 1: Auckland to Rotorua

**Morning (Auckland)** Pack the car early. If you're leaving before 8am, you'll miss peak Auckland traffic and make good time. Stop for coffee and breakfast at a cafe in the city (or pack breakfast) before hitting SH1. **Drive: Auckland → Rotorua (3.5 hours, 240km)** The route takes you south through Hamilton (1.5 hours) and then inland to Rotorua. Hamilton is flat and skippable unless you want a leg-stretch stop. The road gets more interesting as you approach Rotorua — the landscape shifts from farmland to geothermal and forest. **Recommended stop:** Lake Rotoiti State Highway Rest Area (2 hours in) — 10-minute leg stretch, restrooms, water. Nothing fancy but essential with kids. **Afternoon (Rotorua arrival, 1–2pm)** Check into accommodation. Most family-friendly options are: - **Top 10 Holiday Park Rotorua** — $140–180 for a cabin with ensuite, has pools and jumping pillows. Book ahead. - **Hilton Lake Rotorua** — $220–320. Better quality, excellent views, easy for toddlers (they have suites with kitchenettes). - **YMCA Motels Rotorua** — $100–150. Budget-friendly, clean, perfect for a stopover. **Optional activity (if energy allows, 3–4pm):** - **Redwoods Treewalk Rotorua** ($35 per adult, $20 kids 6–15) — 1.5-hour guided canopy walk through old-growth forest. Magical and not physically hard. Older kids love this. Takes 2 hours total including safety briefing. OR - **Rotorua Hot Pools** (various, $20–30 per person) — arrive early, soak. Kids love the warm water. Go to Kuirau Park if you want free hot pools (pool-side but outdoor). **Dinner:** Rotorua has excellent restaurants. Try: - **Tamaki Māori Village** (dinner + cultural show, $95 adults/$65 kids) — touristy but kids love the hangi (earth oven) meal and Māori performances. Book ahead. OR - **Ginza Sushi** or **Bubba Gump Shrimp Co** (casual, $70–100 for family) — easier with tired kids, no fuss. **Night:** Settle into accommodation. Most kids are tired from driving. **Day 1 Budget:** - Accommodation: $140–180 - Petrol: $50–60 - Activities: $35–100 (optional) - Food: $100–150 - **Total: $325–590**

Day 2: Full Day in Rotorua

**Morning (8am start)** **Geothermal parks** — this is the reason kids want to come to Rotorua. Choose one: - **Te Puia** ($35 adults/$18 kids) — Pohutu Geyser (erupts every 1–2 hours, reliable), hot springs, Māori arts centre, kiwi bird sanctuary. 2–3 hours. Best all-rounder. - **Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland** ($39 adults/$20 kids) — more dramatic landscapes (Champagne Pool, Paint Pot), fewer crowds than Te Puia. 2.5 hours. Better for scenic photos; fewer "wow" moments for young kids. - **Rotorua Museum** ($15 adults/$7 kids) — inside the Rotorua Government Gardens, covers geothermal history, less dramatic but educational for older kids. **Recommendation:** Te Puia for first-time visitors with young kids. The geyser eruption never fails to impress. **Lunch (12:30pm):** Packed lunch near Te Puia gardens or head to town for: - **Skyline Rotorua Cafe** — midway lunch, lakeside views, casual ($50–80 family) - **BayExpresso** — good coffee and kids' meals, central Rotorua **Afternoon (1:30–5pm)** **Option A (for active kids 6+):** - **Roto-Vegas Luge** ($40 adults/$30 kids per ride, 3 rides=$100–130 per person) — bobsled-style luge rides down a track through forest. Kids aged 5+ can ride independently. Takes 2 hours including waiting. Pure adrenaline. Book ahead in school holidays. **Option B (for younger kids or tired families):** - **Redwoods Canopy Tours** (if not done Day 1) — $35–45 adults, $20–25 kids. Immersive, beautiful, no rush. - **Rainbow Springs Nature Park** ($35 adults/$20 kids) — trout fishing, native birds, kiwi sanctuary, hot pools. More relaxed, great for kids who like animals. 2 hours. - **Rotorua Lake Cruises** ($40 adults/$25 kids) — 1-hour cruise on Lake Rotorua, scenic, low-key. **Dinner (6pm):** After a full activity day, kids will be tired. Consider casual: - **Skyline Rotorua** — dinner + gondola/luge combo. Splurge option. Book ahead. - **Local pizza** — **Volcano Pizzas** or **Classico** — under $80 for family, quick, kids love it. - **Hobbiton Movie Set** — if energy allows and you haven't visited before ($99 adults/$50 kids). Requires a 20-minute shuttle from town. Takes 2.5 hours. Best done earlier in the day, but evening bookings available. Magical for Lord of Rings fans. **Night:** Early bed. Kids are exhausted. **Day 2 Budget:** - Accommodation: $140–180 - Geothermal park: $35–80 - Lunch: $50–80 - Luge or activity: $100–150 - Dinner: $70–100 - **Total: $395–590**

Day 3: Rotorua to Auckland (Drive Home)

**Morning (7:30am departure)** Early start saves time and avoids afternoon traffic. Pack breakfast in the car or stop in Hamilton (1.5 hours out) for a cafe breakfast. **Optional quick stop (8:30–9:30am, if time allows):** If you haven't done the luge or had enough of Rotorua, visit Kuirau Park for free geothermal features and hot pools ($8 entry, 30 minutes) before departing. Otherwise skip and drive straight. **Drive home (3.5 hours, 240km):** Same route back to Auckland via Hamilton. **Lunch stop:** Mercer (1.5 hours from Rotorua) has service station cafes. Bring snacks to minimize time. **Arrival Auckland (1–2pm):** Home in time for a quiet afternoon. **Day 3 Budget:** - Accommodation: $0 (checking out) - Petrol: $50–60 - Breakfast/lunch: $40–60 - Optional stop: $8–30 - **Total: $98–150**

Packing for 3-Day Rotorua Trip

  • **Swimwear + towels** — essential for hot pools
  • **Layers** — Rotorua can be warm in the day, cool at night. Bring fleece or jumper
  • **Sturdy shoes** — for walking geothermal areas (ground can be uneven and hot)
  • **Hat and sunscreen SPF50+** — UV index is high. Non-negotiable
  • **Rain jacket** — Rotorua is geothermally active and weather can change fast
  • **Merino wool layers** — warm, dries fast if you end up in pools
  • **Casual dinner clothes** — you'll eat out; bring something nicer than driving clothes
  • **Entertainment for the car** — headphones, downloaded shows, audiobooks, books
  • **First aid kit** — bandages, painkillers, antihistamines for bites
  • **Portable charger** — phone/tablet battery for long drives
  • **Snacks for the road** — nuts, fruit, crackers (service stations are expensive)

Driving Tips (Auckland to Rotorua)

  • **Depart early (before 8am)** to avoid Auckland rush hour traffic
  • **Stop every 90 minutes** — kids get restless. Lake Rotoiti rest area is perfect 2-hour mark
  • **Download audiobooks/podcasts** — long drives go faster with good audio content
  • **Pack snacks** — never rely on service stations for food timing
  • **Check fuel levels** — rural stretches between Hamilton and Rotorua have limited stations
  • **SH1 and SH5 are well-maintained** — no mountain passes, straightforward drive
  • **Avoid school holidays if possible** — SH1 can be very busy mid-July and during Dec/Jan
  • **Kids under 7 struggle beyond 3.5 hours** — consider stopping near Hamilton overnight if leaving late
  • **Navigation:** Use Google Maps offline download of the route (cell coverage is good but reassuring to have offline backup)

Booking Tips & Timing

  • **School holidays:** Book accommodation 4–6 weeks ahead. Luge and activities book out.
  • **Off-season (Feb–May, Sep–Nov):** Book 2–3 weeks ahead. Activities have shorter queues.
  • **Winter (Jun–Aug):** Similar to school holidays — book ahead for accommodation.
  • **Hotelpod app** — Find last-minute deals on Rotorua accommodation
  • **Hobbiton** — Book online in advance; walk-ups rarely available in peak season
  • **Luge** — Book online or arrive early in the day to minimize queues
  • **Holiday parks** — TOP 10 loyalty membership (free) gives small discounts
  • **Geothermal parks** — No booking needed; arrive early (8am) to beat crowds
