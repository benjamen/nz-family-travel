How Much Does a NZ Family Holiday Cost? — 2026 Budget Guide

New Zealand is not a cheap destination, but it's not as expensive as most families fear — if you plan right. Here's a detailed 2026 cost breakdown for a family of 4.

Accommodation Costs

This is where campervans make the biggest difference. Holiday parks in NZ charge $25–40 per night for a powered site (campervan) vs $150–400 per night for hotel rooms. Over 10 nights, that's a saving of $1,250–3,600.

Activity Costs

Activities are the biggest wildcard in your NZ budget. Free activities (Huka Falls, Queenstown Gardens, DOC walks) are genuinely excellent. Paid activities range from $14 (Wai-O-Tapu child entry) to $500+ (helicopter at Franz Josef). Budget $100–250/day for activities for a family of 4.

Food Costs

NZ restaurant meals cost $15–25 per main for adults. A family lunch at a café runs $60–100. The savings from cooking your own meals (campervan or self-catering) are significant — $40–80/meal compared to $100–160 eating out.
  • Countdown/Pak'nSave supermarkets: cheapest groceries. Pak'nSave is 10–15% cheaper than Countdown.
  • Petrol station pies ($3–5): a NZ institution. Handy on road trips.
  • Fergburger, Queenstown: iconic burger. Expect queues — go at 5pm or after 9pm.
  • Eat Streat, Rotorua: great affordable food court atmosphere.
  • Holiday park communal kitchens: free to use. Shared with other travellers — fine for most meals.

Fuel Costs

NZ petrol prices run $2.40–2.80 per litre in 2026. A 10-day South Island trip covers roughly 1,400km. A rental car averages 8–10L/100km; a campervan averages 12–15L/100km. Budget $300–450 for fuel on a 10-day South Island trip in a campervan.

How to Reduce Your NZ Family Holiday Cost

  • Travel in March or November (shoulder season) — 15–30% cheaper than January.
  • Use a campervan for 7+ day trips — saves $800–1500 on accommodation.
  • Book activities via Bookme.co.nz — up to 40% discount on many Rotorua/Queenstown activities.
  • Cook 70% of your meals — saves $40–80/day for a family of 4.
  • Use DOC walks and free attractions — Huka Falls, Queenstown foreshore, Christchurch Botanic Gardens.
  • Buy groceries at Pak'nSave rather than New World or Countdown.
  • Freedom camp (self-contained campervans only) — saves $25–40/night on park fees.
