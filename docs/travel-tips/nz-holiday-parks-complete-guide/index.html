NZ Holiday Parks — The Complete Family Guide (TOP 10 vs Freedom Camping)

When planning a family road trip in New Zealand, choosing the right accommodation is crucial. With over 300 holiday parks to choose from, it can be overwhelming. Here's a comprehensive guide to help you decide between TOP 10 holiday parks and freedom camping, including the best parks by region and essential booking tips.

TOP 10 Holiday Parks vs Freedom Camping

TOP 10 holiday parks offer a range of facilities, including powered sites, cabins, and communal kitchens, while freedom camping is a more budget-friendly option that allows you to park your campervan or tent in designated areas. However, freedom camping has its limitations, with limited access to amenities and restrictions on where you can camp.

Facilities and Amenities

  • Powered and non-powered sites
  • Cabins and motel units
  • Communal kitchens and BBQ areas
  • Laundry facilities and dump stations
  • Playgrounds and kids' activities
  • Wi-Fi and TV lounges

Cost Comparison

Accommodation TypePrice (NZD)
TOP 10 Holiday Park powered site40-60
TOP 10 Holiday Park cabin120-200
Freedom camping0-10
Department of Conservation (DoC) campsite10-20

Booking Tips

It's essential to book your holiday park or campsite in advance, especially during peak season (December to February). You can book online through the holiday park's website or through a booking platform like Booking.com. Be sure to read reviews and check the cancellation policies before making a booking. Prices start from $40 per night for a powered site at a TOP 10 holiday park, while a cabin can cost between $120-200 per night.

Best Holiday Parks by Region

  • North Island: Wellington TOP 10 Holiday Park, Bay of Islands Holiday Park
  • South Island: Queenstown Lakeview Holiday Park, Franz Josef TOP 10 Holiday Park
  • Coromandel: Hahei Holiday Resort, Whangamata Beach Hop Holiday Park

Drive Times and Distances

When planning your itinerary, be sure to consider drive times and distances between destinations. For example, the drive from Auckland to Rotorua takes around 3 hours, while the drive from Queenstown to Wanaka takes around 1 hour.
