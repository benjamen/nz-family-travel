Best Road Trip Snacks for Kids in NZ — No-Mess, No-Meltdown Options

A long drive through New Zealand's stunning landscapes can be a dream come true, but not if your kids are hangry. With a 4-hour drive from Auckland to Rotorua or a 6-hour drive from Queenstown to Franz Josef, it's essential to pack the right snacks. To avoid meltdowns and messy cars, we've put together this guide to the best road trip snacks for kids in NZ.

Practical Snack Ideas for Long Drives

When choosing snacks for your road trip, consider options that are easy to eat, won't make a mess, and can withstand temperature changes. Fresh fruit, granola bars, and trail mix are all great choices. You can also try making your own snacks, such as energy balls or sandwiches, to save money and ensure they meet your kids' dietary needs.

Supermarket Options

  • Pak'nSave's $2.50 loaf of wholemeal bread
  • Countdown's $3.99 packet of Arnott's Shapes
  • New World's $4.99 container of hummus and crackers

Cooler Tips

A cooler is essential for keeping perishable snacks fresh during long drives. Consider investing in a portable cooler with insulation to keep items cool for up to 12 hours. You can find these at outdoor stores like Kathmandu or Macpac for around $50-$100. Don't forget to pack ice packs and plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Avoiding Meltdowns

In addition to packing the right snacks, it's essential to take regular breaks during long drives. Try stopping every 2 hours at a rest stop or playground to let your kids stretch their legs and burn off energy. You can also bring along entertainment, such as audiobooks or portable games, to keep them occupied during the drive.

Popular Road Trip Routes

Some popular road trip routes in NZ include the drive from Auckland to Bay of Islands (around 3 hours) or the scenic route from Queenstown to Milford Sound (around 4 hours). Be sure to check the road conditions and weather forecast before embarking on your journey and pack accordingly.
