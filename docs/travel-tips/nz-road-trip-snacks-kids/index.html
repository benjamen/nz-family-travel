Best Road Trip Snacks for Kids in NZ — No-Mess, No-Meltdown Options

Embarking on a long road trip with kids in NZ can be challenging, especially when it comes to snacks. A well-stocked snack stash can save the day, or at least the drive from Auckland to Rotorua, which takes around 3 hours. With a little planning, you can keep your kids happy and satisfied on the road.

Choosing the Right Snacks

When selecting snacks for your road trip, consider non-perishable items that are easy to eat on-the-go. Look for snacks that are low in sugar, mess-free, and can withstand temperature changes. Some great options include nuts, dried fruits, and energy bars. You can find these snacks at most supermarkets, such as Countdown or New World, for around $2-$5 per pack.

Supermarket Snack Options

  • Countdown's brand of trail mix ($3.50 per pack)
  • New World's organic dried fruit range ($4.99 per pack)
  • Pak'nSave's energy bars ($2.99 per pack)

Cooler Tips

If you plan to bring perishable snacks like sandwiches, fruits, or cheese, invest in a good cooler to keep them fresh. Fill the cooler with ice packs and place it in a shaded area to maintain the temperature. This is especially important during long drives, such as the 10-hour journey from Auckland to Wellington.

Avoiding Meltdowns

To avoid meltdowns, make sure to take regular breaks during your road trip. Stop at rest stops or parks to let your kids stretch their legs and burn off energy. You can also create a road trip playlist with their favorite songs to keep them entertained. Additionally, consider investing in a portable DVD player or tablet to keep them occupied during long drives.
