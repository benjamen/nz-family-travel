Te Anau for Families — Overview

Te Anau is the main town in Fiordland National Park and the essential base for visiting Milford Sound. The town sits on the shores of Lake Te Anau — the largest lake in the South Island. Beyond Milford, Te Anau itself has excellent family attractions including the Te Ana-au Caves (glow worms), the Wildlife Centre (free, with kākāpō viewing), and excellent lakeside dining.

Top Things to Do in Te Anau with Kids

  • ✓ Milford Sound day trip — 2-hour boat cruise from Milford. Drive from Te Anau is 2 hrs each way — spectacular.
  • ✓ Te Ana-au Caves (Real Journeys) — glow worm cave tour by boat and walk. 1.5 hrs. $55/adult, $25/child.
  • ✓ Te Anau Wildlife Centre (DOC) — free; see kākāpō (world's rarest parrot), kiwi, weka, and takahē
  • ✓ Lake Te Anau foreshore — kayak hire, water activities, stunning mountain views
  • ✓ Kepler Track — shorter sections suitable for families (the first 6km to the lake is flat and scenic)
  • ✓ Fiordland Cinema — excellent 45-min film about Fiordland, good rainy day option

Te Anau Family FAQ

Is it better to base in Te Anau or Queenstown for Milford Sound?

Te Anau is much better as a Milford base. The drive from Te Anau to Milford is 2 hours (spectacular). From Queenstown it's 4.5 hours — a very long day trip. Stay in Te Anau for at least 2 nights: one day for the glow worm caves and Wildlife Centre, one day for Milford Sound.

Are the Te Ana-au Caves suitable for young children?

Yes — the glow worm cave tour is excellent for children as young as 4. The cave is accessible (no tight squeezes), the boat section on an underground lake is magical, and the glow worms are genuinely spectacular. The tour takes about 1.5 hours and involves some walking. Bring a warm layer — caves are 12°C year-round.
