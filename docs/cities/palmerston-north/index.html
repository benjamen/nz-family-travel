Palmerston North for Families — Overview

Palmerston North (Palmy) is a university city in the centre of the North Island. For families, the key drawcard is Te Manawa — a combined museum, art gallery, and science centre that's outstanding for school-age kids. The Victoria Esplanade is a beautiful park with a miniature railway, aviary, and botanic gardens. It's an easy stopover on the Auckland–Wellington journey.

Top Things to Do in Palmerston North with Kids

  • ✓ Te Manawa Museum — free entry; excellent science and natural history exhibits for kids
  • ✓ Victoria Esplanade — miniature railway (weekends), botanic gardens, aviary, playground. Free.
  • ✓ Lido Aquatic Centre — indoor/outdoor pools, waterslide. Family favourite.
  • ✓ New World Cup Rugby Museum — if your kids love rugby
  • ✓ Manawatu Gorge scenic drive (30 min east) — dramatic river gorge, DOC walks
  • ✓ Linton Military Camp museum (for older kids interested in NZ military history)

Palmerston North Family FAQ

Is Te Manawa worth visiting?

Yes — Te Manawa is one of NZ's best regional museums for families. The combined museum, art gallery, and science centre approach means there's something for every age. Entry is completely free. The interactive science exhibits are particularly good for school-age children. Allow 2–3 hours.

Is Palmerston North worth stopping at between Auckland and Wellington?

Yes, if you're road-tripping the North Island. It breaks the drive nicely (about 2 hours from Wellington), Te Manawa gives you a solid half-day, and the Victoria Esplanade with its miniature railway is great for kids who need to run around after a long drive.
