Invercargill for Families — Overview

Invercargill is often dismissed as a transit stop for Stewart Island or Fiordland, but the city has genuinely excellent family attractions. Tuatāra Tuatara Sanctuary Te Ara Parata (the world's largest tuatara display) at the Southland Museum is remarkable. The Burt Munro Indian motorcycle collection is a NZ legend. And Bluff oysters (in season) are a serious reason to visit in their own right.

Top Things to Do in Invercargill with Kids

  • ✓ Tuatāra Tuatara Sanctuary (Southland Museum) — world's largest captive tuatara display. Free.
  • ✓ E. Hayes & Sons Hardware Store — Burt Munro's actual 1920 Indian Scout motorcycle plus extraordinary collection. Free.
  • ✓ Bill Richardson Transport World — NZ's largest private vehicle collection. $25/adult.
  • ✓ Queens Park — large city park with playground, rose gardens, aviary, and mini golf. Free.
  • ✓ Bluff Oyster and Food Festival (May) — the world's best flat oysters, celebrated with a food festival in nearby Bluff
  • ✓ Stewart Island ferry from Bluff (30 min south) — day trip or overnight to one of NZ's most special islands

Invercargill Family FAQ

What are tuatara and why see them in Invercargill?

Tuatara are ancient NZ reptiles — living fossils that have barely changed in 250 million years. They're found nowhere else in the world. Invercargill's Tuatara Sanctuary has the world's largest captive display, with tuatara of various ages including hatchlings. Entry is free. It's one of the best places in NZ to see and learn about these remarkable animals up close.
