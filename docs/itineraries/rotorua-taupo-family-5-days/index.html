Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1

Arrive Rotorua — Wai-O-Tapu & Polynesian Spa

📍 Rotorua 🚗 230km from Auckland (2.5h) or 85km from Tauranga (1h) 💰 ~$280
  • Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland — Lady Knox Geyser at 10:15am daily (book this!)
  • Champagne Pool, Artist's Palette, and colourful hydrothermal pools
  • Check in and relax
  • Evening: Polynesian Spa pools (family pool, $25/child, $38/adult)
Tips:
  • Wai-O-Tapu: $45/adult, $18/child — buy online to save 10%
  • Lady Knox Geyser erupts at exactly 10:15am every day (artificially triggered with soap)
  • Rotorua smells of sulphur everywhere — warn the kids, it fades in 20 minutes
🏕️ Stay: Rotorua TOP 10 Holiday Park or Wylie Court Motor Lodge Find accommodation →
Day 2

Te Puia & Whakarewarewa Village

📍 Rotorua 🚗 0km 💰 ~$350
  • Te Puia Geothermal Park — Pohutu Geyser (NZ's largest), kiwi house, Maori carving school
  • Cultural performance at Te Puia (included in entry)
  • Afternoon: Rotorua Museum and government gardens (architecturally stunning)
  • Evening: Mitai Maori Village or Tamaki Maori Village cultural dinner ($95-130/adult)
Tips:
  • Te Puia: $60/adult, $30/child — allow 3 hours
  • Pohutu Geyser erupts frequently — check signage at entrance for recent activity
  • Mitai is more family-friendly than Tamaki (earlier finish time)
🏕️ Stay: Rotorua TOP 10 Holiday Park Find accommodation →
Day 3

Hobbiton Movie Set

📍 Matamata (1hr from Rotorua) 🚗 70km from Rotorua (1h) 💰 ~$380
  • Hobbiton Movie Set tour — 2-hour guided tour of the Shire from Lord of the Rings
  • Green Dragon Inn — included beer/cider/ginger beer at end of tour
  • Afternoon: return to Rotorua or continue to Taupo
  • Option: Agrodome Farm Show in Rotorua (if returning) — sheep shearing, working dogs, $45/adult
Tips:
  • Hobbiton: $55/adult, $27/child, $167 family (2+2) — book at least 4-6 weeks ahead
  • The tour is 2 hours on foot — bring snacks for young kids
  • Matamata Visitor Centre runs the shuttle — no need to drive the farm roads yourself
🏕️ Stay: Taupo TOP 10 Holiday Park (move to Taupo tonight) Find accommodation →
Day 4

Lake Taupo & Huka Falls

📍 Taupo 🚗 85km from Rotorua (1h) or 0km if moved last night 💰 ~$250
  • Huka Falls — most visited natural site in NZ, 30min walk from car park (free)
  • Wairakei Terraces Geothermal Pools — silica terraces and hot pools ($35/adult, $20/child)
  • Lunch at Lake Taupo foreshore — great playground nearby
  • Taupo DeBretts Spa Resort pools — outdoor thermal family pools
  • Optional: jet boat on the Waikato River ($60-90/adult)
Tips:
  • Huka Falls is genuinely spectacular — the most water per second of any NZ waterfall
  • Taupo playground at the lakefront is excellent for toddlers — right on the beach
  • If kids are older: lake cruises to the Maori Rock Carvings ($45/adult)
🏕️ Stay: Taupo TOP 10 Holiday Park Find accommodation →
Day 5

Tongariro & Depart

📍 Tongariro National Park / Whakapapa 🚗 75km from Taupo (1h) 💰 ~$120
  • Whakapapa Village — visitor centre, views of Ruapehu and Ngauruhoe (Mt Doom)
  • Taranaki Falls walk (2hr return, family-friendly, waterfall through lava fields)
  • Silica Rapids walk (2.5hr, through geothermal landscape, manageable for kids 7+)
  • Drive south to Wellington or return north
Tips:
  • Taranaki Falls is suitable for kids 5+ — well-formed track, no technical sections
  • Tongariro Alpine Crossing is adult-only (full day, challenging) — not suitable for families with young kids
  • Weather changes fast on the mountain — pack rain jackets even in summer

FAQ

How many days in Rotorua is enough for families?

2 full days covers the core sites (Wai-O-Tapu, Te Puia, evening cultural show). Add a third day for Hobbiton if that's a priority. 5 days including Taupo and Tongariro is the full circuit and well worth it.

Is Hobbiton worth the money for kids?

Yes for kids who know Lord of the Rings or The Hobbit (ages 8+). For younger kids it's still a beautiful farm walk with fun hobbit holes. The included drink at the Green Dragon at the end is a highlight for adults.

What is the best geothermal park in Rotorua?

Wai-O-Tapu is the most colourful and varied — the Artist's Palette and Champagne Pool are extraordinary. Te Puia has the best geyser (Pohutu) and includes Maori cultural content. For families, visit both if budget allows.
