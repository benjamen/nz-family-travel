Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1

Auckland to Paihia — The Bay

📍 Paihia, Bay of Islands 🚗 245km from Auckland (3h) 💰 ~$200
  • Drive north via Wellsford and Whangarei
  • Stop at Whangarei Falls (15min walk, free) on the way through
  • Arrive Paihia — check in, walk the foreshore
  • Late afternoon swim at Paihia Beach
  • Waitangi Treaty Grounds are walkable from Paihia foreshore (visit Day 2)
Tips:
  • Paihia TOP 10 Holiday Park is the best family base — walk to everything
  • Book dolphin cruise for Day 2 morning from Paihia wharf
  • Fuel up in Whangarei — Bay of Islands prices are higher
🏕️ Stay: Paihia TOP 10 Holiday Park or Paihia Beach accommodation Find accommodation →
Day 2

Dolphin Cruise & Waitangi

📍 Bay of Islands 🚗 0km 💰 ~$320
  • Dolphin Discoveries cruise — swim with dolphins or watch from boat (book ahead)
  • Hole in the Rock passage — dramatic sea arch at Cape Brett
  • Afternoon: Waitangi Treaty Grounds — NZ's founding document site, cultural performances
  • Evening: Paihia foreshore restaurants
Tips:
  • Dolphin Discoveries dolphin swim is $89/adult, $55/child — book online
  • Waitangi is $40/adult, kids free — allow 2 hours
  • Paihia waterfront cafes are good value for lunch
🏕️ Stay: Paihia TOP 10 Holiday Park Find accommodation →
Day 3

Kerikeri & Cape Reinga Road

📍 Kerikeri / heading north 🚗 20km Paihia to Kerikeri 💰 ~$150
  • Kerikeri: Rainbow Falls walk (30min return, free, beautiful)
  • Kerikeri Mission Station — NZ's oldest surviving building (1822)
  • Drive north to Kaitaia — base for Cape Reinga next day
  • Option: Doubtless Bay detour for a swim at Coopers Beach
Tips:
  • Coopers Beach is one of Northland's best — worth the small detour
  • Kaitaia is not scenic — use it only as a fuel and sleep stop
  • Stock up on food in Kaitaia — no supermarkets north of here
🏕️ Stay: Kaitaia motel or continue to Ahipara holiday park Find accommodation →
Day 4

Cape Reinga & 90 Mile Beach

📍 Cape Reinga / Te Paki 🚗 105km from Kaitaia (1.5h) 💰 ~$100
  • Cape Reinga Lighthouse — walk to NZ's spiritual northern tip (30min return)
  • Watch the Tasman Sea and Pacific Ocean collide — extraordinary
  • Te Paki Sand Dunes — hire boards at the gate ($20) and surf down 100m dunes
  • Ninety Mile Beach — drive on beach with a 4WD (do NOT attempt in a regular rental car)
  • Return via inland road through Te Paki farms
Tips:
  • Sand dune boards are available at the car park for $20 — worth every cent for kids
  • Do NOT drive on Ninety Mile Beach in a rental car — hire car insurance is void on beaches
  • Take a organised Cape Reinga bus tour from Kaitaia if you want beach driving experience
🏕️ Stay: Ahipara Holiday Park or return to Kaitaia Find accommodation →
Day 5

Waipoua Forest & Drive South

📍 Waipoua Forest / Dargaville 🚗 170km from Kaitaia (2h) 💰 ~$80
  • Waipoua Forest — Tane Mahuta, the world's largest living kauri tree (15min walk, free)
  • Te Matua Ngahere — the 'Father of the Forest' kauri (40min return walk)
  • Trounson Kauri Park — evening kiwi spotting tour option
  • Dargaville Museum — fascinating Northland and kauri history
  • Drive south via Dargaville to Auckland (3h)
Tips:
  • Tane Mahuta is unmissable — genuinely awe-inspiring, all ages
  • You must stay on the boardwalks — kauri dieback disease kills trees via foot contact
  • Allow an early start to reach Auckland by early evening
🏕️ Stay: Back in Auckland or continue south Find accommodation →

FAQ

Is the Bay of Islands trip doable with toddlers?

Yes — Paihia beach is calm and safe, the dolphin cruise is exciting for toddlers, and Waitangi has good open space. Cape Reinga is a long drive for a toddler but the sand dunes are worth it. A campervan gives flexibility for nap stops.

How far is Auckland to the Bay of Islands?

245km (about 3 hours) via State Highway 1. Traffic out of Auckland can add 30-45 minutes Friday evenings. Most families stop at Whangarei Falls halfway.

Should I do a Cape Reinga bus tour or drive myself?

Drive yourself if you have a rental car or campervan — you have more flexibility. Take a bus tour if you want the 90 Mile Beach driving experience (insurance voids on personal vehicles). Tours from Kaitaia are around $60-70 per adult.
