Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1

City — Botanic Gardens, Gondola & Punting

📍 Christchurch city 🚗 Arrive Christchurch 💰 ~$280
  • Christchurch Botanic Gardens — free, stunning, great playground and cafe
  • Punting on the Avon — 30-minute punt through the gardens ($35/adult, under 5 free)
  • Christchurch Gondola — cable car to Summit Station, Port Hills views ($35/adult, $19/child)
  • New Regent Street for dinner — NZ's most picturesque street, several family-friendly restaurants
Tips:
  • Botanic Gardens is genuinely excellent — the playground near the fountain is one of Christchurch's best
  • Book punting online — peak season sells out midday slots
  • Gondola summit has a Time Tunnel exhibition — kids enjoy it

Day 2

Willowbank Wildlife Reserve & Beach

📍 Christchurch / New Brighton 🚗 0km 💰 ~$260
  • Willowbank Wildlife Reserve — NZ native birds, kiwi encounter, farm animals ($30/adult, $16/child)
  • Te Puia o Waikawa (Christchurch Thermal Springs) if open
  • New Brighton Beach and Pier — iconic long pier, free hot sea pools, foreshore playground
  • New Brighton hot pools — free outdoor seawater pools right on the beach
Tips:
  • Willowbank is smaller and less visited than Wellington Zoo but excellent — see kiwi in daylight
  • New Brighton hot pools are free and genuinely warm — kids go wild
  • New Brighton pier is 300m long — great for fishing or just the walk
🏕️ Stay: Christchurch TOP 10 Holiday Park Find accommodation →
Day 3

Akaroa or Kaikoura Day Trip

📍 Akaroa or Kaikoura 🚗 80km to Akaroa (1h) or 180km to Kaikoura (2h) 💰 ~$320
  • Option A — Akaroa: French colonial village, swim with dolphins ($145/adult, $105/child), Giant's House garden
  • Option B — Kaikoura: Whale Watch or seal colony walk, peninsula walkway, fresh crayfish
  • Return to Christchurch for evening flight or overnight
Tips:
  • Akaroa is closer and more relaxed — ideal for younger kids
  • Kaikoura is better for older kids (whale watch, sea kayaking with seals)
  • Akaroa swim-with-dolphins: book 2-3 weeks ahead in summer

FAQ

Is Christchurch good for families?

Yes — it's improved dramatically since the 2011 earthquake and now has some of NZ's best family attractions. The Botanic Gardens, gondola, punting, and New Brighton hot pools are all excellent. It's also a great base for Kaikoura and Akaroa day trips.

What happened to Christchurch after the earthquakes?

The February 2011 earthquake (185 deaths) destroyed much of the CBD. The city has been substantially rebuilt with a modern, open-plan centre, the Cardboard Cathedral, and excellent public spaces. The rebuilding story is itself interesting for older kids to learn about.
