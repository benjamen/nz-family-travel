Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1

Fly into Christchurch — Explore the City

📍 Christchurch 🚗 Arrive Christchurch Airport 💰 ~$180
  • Punting on the Avon — relaxing, beautiful, $30-35/adult, kids often free
  • Botanic Gardens — free, excellent playground, cafe
  • Christchurch Gondola — cable car ride to Port Hills with harbour views ($35/adult, $19/child)
  • New Regent Street for dinner — colourful Art Deco street
Tips:
  • Pick up rental car at airport — Christchurch has great car hire competition, book early for best rates
  • Christchurch is very driveable — no traffic issues outside peak hours
  • New Brighton beach (20min east) has a long pier and free hot pools
🏕️ Stay: Christchurch TOP 10 Holiday Park ($100-120/night) Find accommodation →
Day 2

Kaikoura — Whale Watch & Seals

📍 Kaikoura 🚗 180km from Christchurch (2h) 💰 ~$380
  • Whale Watch Kaikoura — sperm whale tour ($170/adult, $70/child — budget item but worth it)
  • Or: Seal colony walk at Point Kean (free, hundreds of fur seals up close)
  • Kaikoura Peninsula Walkway (2.5hr loop, seal colony, dramatic coastline)
  • Swim or coastal walk at Kaikoura beach
Tips:
  • Whale Watch is the premium experience — book 4-6 weeks ahead, 90% sighting rate
  • If Whale Watch is over budget, the Seal Colony walk is outstanding and free
  • Kaikoura Seafood BBQ on the foreshore: crayfish, green-lipped mussels, $20-50/person
🏕️ Stay: Kaikoura TOP 10 Holiday Park ($90-110/night) Find accommodation →
Day 3

Lewis Pass to Hokitika

📍 Hokitika / West Coast 🚗 320km via Lewis Pass (4h) 💰 ~$80
  • Lewis Pass Scenic Reserve — short walks in beech forest (free)
  • Springs Junction: Maruia Falls (10min return walk, impressive)
  • Arrive Hokitika — sunset at Hokitika Beach (dramatic driftwood art installations)
Tips:
  • This is a long drive day — leave Kaikoura before 9am
  • Hokitika Gorge is 30min inland — save it for tomorrow morning before driving south
  • Stock up on groceries in Hokitika — Franz Josef is expensive
🏕️ Stay: Hokitika Holiday Park ($80-100/night) Find accommodation →
Day 4

Hokitika Gorge & Franz Josef

📍 Franz Josef 🚗 140km (1.5h) 💰 ~$120
  • Hokitika Gorge — early morning for best turquoise water colour (25min, free)
  • Drive south along the Coast
  • Franz Josef Glacier valley walk — 3km return, free
  • Franz Josef Hot Pools evening ($30/adult, $20/child)
Tips:
  • The hot pools after a glacier walk day is a family highlight
  • Self-cater dinner in Franz Josef — restaurants are pricey
  • Franz Josef village has a small Four Square — limited stock, bring supplies
🏕️ Stay: Franz Josef TOP 10 Holiday Park ($90-120/night) Find accommodation →
Day 5

Haast Pass & Wanaka

📍 Wanaka 🚗 180km via Haast Pass (3h) 💰 ~$100
  • Fox Glacier — Lake Matheson reflection walk (45min return, best before 9am)
  • Haast Pass drive — Thunder Creek Falls (10min return walk, free)
  • Arrive Wanaka — lake beach and town playground
  • Evening: stroll to That Wanaka Tree and lakefront
Tips:
  • Lake Matheson is best in early morning light — try to leave Franz Josef by 7am
  • Haast is remote — fill up on fuel at Fox Glacier
  • Wanaka lake beach is safe for kids and beautiful for a late afternoon swim
🏕️ Stay: Wanaka TOP 10 Holiday Park ($100-120/night) Find accommodation →
Day 6

Mt Cook Day Trip

📍 Mt Cook (day trip from Wanaka or en route to Queenstown) 🚗 185km from Wanaka via Cromwell (2.5h) 💰 ~$80
  • Aoraki/Mt Cook National Park — Hooker Valley Track (3hr return, glacier lake, swing bridges)
  • Mt Cook Village visitor centre
  • Lake Pukaki viewpoint — intense turquoise glacial lake with Mt Cook reflection
  • Continue to Queenstown via Cromwell and Gibbston Valley (1.5h)
Tips:
  • Hooker Valley Track is suitable for kids 6+ — well-formed, flat, magnificent
  • The swing bridges on the Hooker Valley Track are highlights for kids
  • Lake Pukaki viewpoint is the most photographed spot in South Island — stop for 20 minutes
🏕️ Stay: Queenstown TOP 10 Holiday Park ($110-130/night) Find accommodation →
Day 7

Queenstown — Best Value Day

📍 Queenstown 🚗 0km 💰 ~$280
  • Skyline Luge — 3+ runs per person ($30-50/person, best family value in Queenstown)
  • Queenstown Gardens — free, great playground, disc golf
  • Arrowtown day trip (25min) — gold panning $8/person, historic village, river walk
  • Frankton foreshore cycle trail (free, flat, 4km lake loop)
  • Departure or final night in Queenstown
Tips:
  • Skyline Luge: buy 5-run passes for better value
  • Arrowtown has excellent fudge shops and a great playground near the river
  • Budget airline flights from Queenstown to Auckland or Wellington for departure

Packing Tips for This Trip

  • Reusable water bottles — save money on drinks throughout
  • Self-catering supplies for at least half your meals — saves $400+ over 7 days
  • Waterproofs — West Coast and Queenstown can be wet
  • Layers for Mt Cook — temperature can be 10°C colder than coast
  • Sunscreen — Canterbury and Central Otago UV is very high

FAQ

How do we keep costs under $3,000 for a family of 4?

Three strategies: (1) Self-cater at least breakfast and dinner every day — saves $400-600 over 7 nights. (2) Use holiday parks instead of motels — saves $200-300 total. (3) Choose free activities (glacier walks, seal colonies, gorge walks) over paid ones where the experience is comparable.

Is a rental car cheaper than a campervan for this route?

A rental car plus holiday parks is typically $200-400 cheaper than a comparable campervan over 7 nights, especially in peak season. The campervan advantage is flexibility and eliminating check-in/check-out — worth it for longer trips of 10+ days.

Is the Kaikoura detour worth adding to a Christchurch-Queenstown trip?

Yes — Kaikoura is one of NZ's best wildlife experiences. The seal colony alone justifies the stop. Whale Watch is expensive but extraordinary. The coastal road between Christchurch and Kaikoura is scenic and breaks up what would otherwise be a long inland drive.
