Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1

Arrival in Auckland

📍 Auckland 💰 ~$50–150
  • Pick up rental car from Auckland Airport
  • Drive into Auckland CBD (45 mins)
  • Kelly Tarlton's Sea Life Aquarium (if arriving early)
  • Walk the waterfront, dinner at the Viaduct
Tips:
  • Book accommodation near the motorway for easy departure next morning
  • Countdown supermarkets are cheapest for groceries
🏕️ Stay: Auckland CBD hotel or holiday park Find accommodation →
Day 2

Drive to Rotorua — Hobbiton Option

📍 Auckland → Rotorua 🚗 2h 30m direct, or 3h 30m via Hobbiton 💰 ~$120–300
  • Option A: Drive direct to Rotorua (2.5h), check in, afternoon at Te Puia
  • Option B: Hobbiton Movie Set detour in Matamata (add 1 hour each way, book ahead at $49/adult)
  • Evening: Dinner in Rotorua CBD
Tips:
  • Hobbiton MUST be pre-booked — often sold out weeks ahead
  • The direct route via Matamata is still beautiful
🏕️ Stay: Rotorua (holiday park or hotel) Find accommodation →
Day 3

Geothermal & Farm Day

📍 Rotorua 💰 ~$180–280
  • Agrodome Farm Show (9:30am — book ahead)
  • Lunch break (Eat Streat Rotorua has great cheap options)
  • Skyline Rotorua Gondola & Luge (afternoon)
  • Kuirau Park free geothermal walk (evening)
Tips:
  • Get the multi-ride luge pass for better value
  • Kuirau Park has free footbaths — let the kids put their feet in
🏕️ Stay: Rotorua (same) Find accommodation →
Day 4

Wai-O-Tapu + Drive to Taupo

📍 Rotorua → Taupo 🚗 1h (via Wai-O-Tapu detour — 30 mins south of Rotorua) 💰 ~$120–200
  • Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland (arrive by 10am for Lady Knox Geyser at 10:15am)
  • Drive to Taupo (1h from Wai-O-Tapu)
  • Check in, then walk to Huka Falls (free, 15 mins from town)
  • Spa Thermal Park evening soak (free natural hot spring)
Tips:
  • Wai-O-Tapu's Lady Knox Geyser erupts at exactly 10:15am — don't miss it
  • Spa Thermal Park has no changing rooms — wear togs under clothes
🏕️ Stay: Taupo (holiday park or hotel) Find accommodation →
Day 5

Huka Jet + Lake Day

📍 Taupo 💰 ~$200–350
  • Huka Jet boat experience (book ahead — 30 mins, leaves from near Huka Falls)
  • Picnic at Acacia Bay (great swimming beach)
  • Aratiatia Rapids (controlled release at 2pm and 4pm in summer — check times)
  • AC Baths thermal pools (affordable family swim)
Tips:
  • Buy groceries for your beach picnic — saves $40+ vs eating out
  • Aratiatia Rapids release times change seasonally — check the sign at the dam
🏕️ Stay: Taupo (same) Find accommodation →
Day 6

Drive Day to Wellington

📍 Taupo → Wellington 🚗 4h direct (or split with Tongariro National Park stop) 💰 ~$30–80
  • Option A: Drive direct to Wellington (4h), check in, walk the waterfront
  • Option B: Stop at Tongariro National Park for 1.5h (dramatic volcanic scenery — see the Chateau)
  • Cable car up to Kelburn (Wellington — $10 return, great views)
  • Dinner on Cuba Street
Tips:
  • The Tongariro/Desert Road (SH1) is dramatic but add 30 mins
  • Wellington waterfront has great free playgrounds
🏕️ Stay: Wellington Find accommodation →
Day 7

Wellington Day + Departure

📍 Wellington 💰 ~$20–150
  • Te Papa Museum (free, world-class — kids love the earthquake simulator)
  • Wellington Cable Car (views over the harbour)
  • Zealandia (optional — native wildlife sanctuary, 2h)
  • Return rental car at Wellington Airport
Tips:
  • Te Papa is free and easily fills 3 hours
  • Book the Interislander ferry in advance if continuing to South Island

Packing Tips for This Trip

  • Rain jacket for everyone — NZ weather changes fast
  • Swimwear and towels (you'll swim multiple times)
  • Reusable water bottles (save $20+/day vs buying water)
  • Snack bag for the car — petrol station food is expensive
  • Sunscreen — NZ UV is intense even on cloudy days

FAQ

Can we do this itinerary in a campervan?

Yes, and it works even better. You save on accommodation ($40–80/night vs $150–350) and can cook your own meals. Campervans also carry everything you need. See our campervan comparison.

Is this itinerary suitable for toddlers?

Yes — most activities on this route work for under-3s. Agrodome, Te Puia, Huka Falls, and Te Papa are all great for toddlers. The Huka Jet requires minimum 3 years/90cm.

What's the best time of year for this trip?

December–February (summer) is peak season — warm, long days, everything open. January is school holidays (very busy). November and March are sweet spots — shoulder prices, fewer crowds, still warm.
