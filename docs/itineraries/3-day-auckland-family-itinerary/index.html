Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1

Auckland City Highlights

📍 Auckland — City & Waterfront 💰 ~$120-200 (Kelly Tarlton's + ferry)
  • Kelly Tarlton's Sea Life Aquarium (allow 2-3 hours — sharks, penguins, stingrays)
  • Walk Mission Bay beach and promenade (free)
  • Drive to Devonport via ferry (25-min ferry from downtown, $15 return/adult, stunning views of the harbour and Sky Tower)
  • Explore Devonport village and climb North Head volcanic viewpoint
  • Return ferry, dinner at the Viaduct Harbour
Tips:
  • Book Kelly Tarlton's online — it's 10-15% cheaper and avoids queuing
  • The Devonport ferry is one of Auckland's best value activities — harbour views are spectacular

Day 2

Volcanoes, Museum, and Markets

📍 Auckland — Volcanic Peaks & Museum 💰 ~$80-150 (museum + food market lunch)
  • Auckland Museum (free entry for NZ residents, $25/adult for visitors) — excellent Maori taonga collection, earthquake simulator, dinosaur gallery
  • Walk through Auckland Domain (free parkland surrounding the museum)
  • Cornwall Park and One Tree Hill — drive up or walk, 360-degree Auckland views
  • Otara or Avondale Flea Market (Saturday only, 6am-12pm, free entry) — best multicultural food market in Auckland
  • Evening: Auckland waterfront walk or dinner in Ponsonby Road
Tips:
  • Auckland Museum has a free volcano and earthquake experience for kids — worth the visit even if you skip the main galleries
  • Ponsonby Road has excellent family-friendly restaurants — Ima, Little Bird, and The Blue Breeze Inn are all great

Day 3

West Coast Day Trip — Piha or Muriwai

📍 Auckland — West Coast Beaches 🚗 45-60 mins drive from central Auckland 💰 ~$30-60 (fuel + lunch)
  • Drive over the Waitakere Ranges to Piha Beach (45 mins) or Muriwai Beach (50 mins)
  • Piha: dramatic black sand surf beach, Lion Rock walk (15 mins up, outstanding views)
  • Muriwai: gannet colony (September-March) — 10,000+ birds nesting on the cliffs, incredible wildlife experience
  • Swim with lifeguards (surf conditions — stay between the flags)
  • Lunch at the Piha Store or Muriwai Beach cafe
  • Drive back for evening flight or continue south to Rotorua
Tips:
  • NEVER swim at Piha or Muriwai without lifeguards present — these are serious surf beaches with rips
  • Gannet colony at Muriwai is free to view — bring binoculars
  • The Waitakere Ranges road is narrow and winding — drive carefully

FAQ

Is Auckland worth spending 3 days in with kids?

Yes, if you use the time well. Auckland itself has good family attractions (Kelly Tarlton's, Auckland Museum, Cornwall Park) but the city's best family experiences are the day trips — Waiheke Island, the West Coast beaches, and Matamata (Hobbiton). 3 days is the right amount of time to do Auckland properly without rushing.

Do you need a car in Auckland with kids?

Yes, for this itinerary. Public transport works well for central Auckland but you need a car to reach the West Coast beaches and West Auckland properly. Hire cars from Auckland Airport are competitive — book in advance.

What is the best Auckland area to stay in for families?

Newmarket or Remuera (close to Cornwall Park and Auckland Domain), or the Viaduct/Wynyard Quarter (waterfront). Avoid Karangahape Road/Eden Terrace for families. North Shore (Takapuna) is good if you want a beach suburb base.
