Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1

Auckland — Aquarium, Zoo & Waterfront

📍 Auckland 🚗 Arrive Auckland Airport 💰 ~$350
  • Kelly Tarlton's SEA LIFE Aquarium — penguins and shark tunnel (book online: $36/adult, $24/child)
  • Mission Bay beach and foreshore playground
  • Auckland waterfront walk — Viaduct Harbour, cloud forest art
  • Or: Auckland Zoo instead of aquarium ($29/adult, $15/child)
Tips:
  • Pick up rental car at airport — Christchurch has great car hire competition, book early
  • Don't try to do both Zoo and Aquarium on day 1 — choose one
  • Mission Bay playground is excellent for jet-lagged kids needing to run
🏕️ Stay: Takapuna Beach Holiday Park or Auckland city hotel Find accommodation →
Day 2

Auckland — Sky Tower & Waiheke

📍 Auckland 🚗 0km 💰 ~$300
  • Sky Tower observation deck — 360 views, glass floor panels ($35/adult, $17/child)
  • Ferry to Waiheke Island — 45min crossing, bikes or buses to beaches ($38 return/person)
  • Palm Beach or Onetangi Beach on Waiheke — excellent safe swimming beaches
  • Stonyridge or Mudbrick Vineyard for a family lunch (both kid-friendly grounds)
Tips:
  • The Waiheke ferry is itself a highlight for kids — good sea views leaving the city
  • Waiheke buses connect to all main beaches from the ferry terminal
  • Buy Auckland Sky Tower + Ferry as a combo if you want both in one day
🏕️ Stay: Auckland (last night before heading north) Find accommodation →
Day 3

Drive North to Bay of Islands

📍 Bay of Islands 🚗 245km from Auckland (3h) 💰 ~$250
  • Whangarei stop: Whangarei Falls (15min walk, free, beautiful waterfall)
  • Whangarei Town Basin for coffee and a stretch
  • Arrive Paihia — foreshore walk, beach, book dolphin cruise
  • Waitangi Treaty Grounds late afternoon if time allows
Tips:
  • Whangarei Falls is the best motorway break in Northland — pool and picnic area
  • Book dolphin cruise for tomorrow morning from Paihia wharf before you leave
  • Paihia is walkable — park at the holiday park and walk to everything
🏕️ Stay: Paihia TOP 10 Holiday Park Find accommodation →
Day 4

Bay of Islands — Dolphins & Cape Reinga

📍 Bay of Islands / Far North 🚗 0km (Paihia) or 190km to Cape Reinga 💰 ~$380
  • Dolphin Discoveries cruise — swim with dolphins from Paihia wharf ($89/adult, $55/child)
  • Option: Cape Reinga day tour by bus from Paihia ($75-90/adult, includes 90 Mile Beach)
  • Or: Self-drive to Kerikeri — Rainbow Falls, Stone Store, Kerikeri foreshore
Tips:
  • The dolphin swim is the Bay of Islands highlight — book it
  • Cape Reinga by bus is better value than driving yourself (includes beach driving)
  • If doing Cape Reinga by bus, that fills the full day — skip Kerikeri to Day 5
🏕️ Stay: Paihia TOP 10 Holiday Park or start drive south (Waipoua area) Find accommodation →
Day 5

Waipoua Kauri Forest & Return to Auckland

📍 Waipoua Forest / return Auckland 🚗 175km from Paihia via Waipoua (2.5h) 💰 ~$100
  • Waipoua Forest: Tane Mahuta — world's largest living kauri tree (15min walk, free, unmissable)
  • Te Matua Ngahere walk if time allows (40min return)
  • Dargaville stop — Dargaville Museum for Northland history
  • Drive south to Auckland via SH12 and SH1 (2.5h from Dargaville)
Tips:
  • Leave Paihia early (7am) to reach Waipoua by 9:30am and Auckland by 2pm
  • Must stay on boardwalks at all kauri trees — kauri dieback disease is serious
  • Tane Mahuta is genuinely awe-inspiring — adults often more moved than kids

FAQ

Is this trip doable with a toddler or baby?

Yes with some adjustments. The dolphin cruise is fine for babies in arms (some operators have age limits — check). Whangarei Falls, Mission Bay, and Tane Mahuta are all pushchair-friendly. Skip Cape Reinga (very long day) and swap for a relaxing Paihia beach day.

What is the best time of year to visit Northland?

November to April — warm enough for swimming, long daylight hours. January and February are peak (crowded and expensive). November and December are the sweet spot — summer conditions without the school holiday crowds.

Is the Waiheke Island detour worth it on a 5-day trip?

Yes if you're visiting in summer (Oct-Apr) and enjoy beaches or food. It adds a half-day and $35-40 per person in ferry costs, but the crossing and the relaxed island atmosphere are genuinely different from the city.
