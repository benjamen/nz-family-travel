Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1

Arrive Queenstown — Luge & Lake

📍 Queenstown 🚗 Arrive Queenstown Airport 💰 ~$350
  • Skyline Gondola and Luge — cable car up, 3+ luge runs each ($40-55/person)
  • Queenstown Gardens — free playground, duck pond, lakefront walk
  • Steamer Wharf for dinner — great lake views, several family-friendly options
Tips:
  • Buy Luge 5-run passes online — saves 15% and skips ticket queues
  • The Gondola ride alone is worth it for the Remarkables view — kids love the top station
  • Queenstown Gardens playground is one of NZ's best — excellent for younger kids
🏕️ Stay: Queenstown TOP 10 Holiday Park Find accommodation →
Day 2

TSS Earnslaw & Arrowtown

📍 Queenstown / Arrowtown 🚗 0km (Queenstown) / 25km to Arrowtown 💰 ~$380
  • TSS Earnslaw steamship lake cruise ($62/adult, $31/child — book 10am departure)
  • Walter Peak High Country Farm tour add-on ($80/adult, includes afternoon tea, well worth it)
  • Afternoon: Arrowtown village (25min drive) — gold panning in the Arrow River ($8/person)
  • Arrowtown Chinese Settlement — NZ's best-preserved gold rush Chinese village, free to explore
Tips:
  • The TSS Earnslaw + Walter Peak combo is a genuine highlight — book it
  • Gold panning in the Arrow River: genuine gold flakes, kids find it every time
  • Arrowtown has excellent ice cream and fudge shops — budget $20 per family member
🏕️ Stay: Queenstown TOP 10 Holiday Park Find accommodation →
Day 3

Drive to Wanaka

📍 Wanaka 🚗 70km from Queenstown (1h) via Crown Range or SH6 💰 ~$300
  • Crown Range Road — NZ's highest sealed road (summer only), extraordinary views
  • Arrive Wanaka — Puzzle World (optical illusions, gravity room, amazing for kids, $24/adult, $18/child)
  • Wanaka lake beach — safe swimming, kayak hire
  • That Wanaka Tree — the famous lake willow, 10min walk from town
Tips:
  • Puzzle World is genuinely excellent for ages 5-14 — allow 2.5 hours
  • Wanaka Beach is calm and safe for all ages in summer
  • Glendhu Bay Motor Camp (20min from town) is the most scenic holiday park in NZ
🏕️ Stay: Wanaka TOP 10 Holiday Park or Glendhu Bay Motor Camp Find accommodation →
Day 4

Mt Cook Day Trip

📍 Aoraki/Mt Cook National Park (day trip from Wanaka) 🚗 185km from Wanaka (2.5h via Cromwell) 💰 ~$150
  • Lake Pukaki viewpoint — turquoise glacial lake with Mt Cook backdrop (free, stunning)
  • Aoraki/Mt Cook Visitor Centre
  • Hooker Valley Track — 3hr return walk to glacier lake and swing bridges
  • Return to Wanaka
Tips:
  • Hooker Valley Track: suitable for kids 6+, well-formed track, three swing bridges, end viewpoint is extraordinary
  • Lake Pukaki stop is mandatory — 20 minutes, one of NZ's most iconic views
  • Leave Wanaka by 7:30am for an early arrival and the best mountain light
🏕️ Stay: Wanaka TOP 10 Holiday Park Find accommodation →
Day 5

Last Day — Lake Wanaka & Depart

📍 Wanaka / Queenstown Airport 🚗 70km Wanaka to Queenstown (1h) 💰 ~$200
  • Morning swim or kayak at Wanaka Beach
  • Roy's Peak optional (serious hike, 5hr return, kids 10+ only, extraordinary views)
  • Or: Rippon Vineyard lawn and lake picnic
  • Drive to Queenstown for departure flight
Tips:
  • Glendhu Bay is a beautiful last-morning coffee stop before driving to Queenstown
  • Queenstown airport is small — arrive 90min before departure
  • If flying home, pack your sandfly repellent in checked baggage

FAQ

Should we base in Queenstown or Wanaka?

Both have great holiday parks. Queenstown has more activity variety and better restaurants for a first night. Wanaka is quieter, more scenic, and better value. Splitting 2 nights in Queenstown and 3 in Wanaka is ideal.

Is Wanaka or Queenstown better for families?

Queenstown has more organised activities (luge, steamship, jet boat) that kids love. Wanaka is more about lakes, nature, and Puzzle World. Both are excellent. Older kids (8+) usually prefer Queenstown; families with younger kids often prefer Wanaka's calmer pace.

Can we do skiing on this 5-day trip?

Yes in winter (June-September). Cardrona is the best family ski field — ski school from age 3, well-organised, 45min from Wanaka. Coronet Peak near Queenstown is steeper and suits older skiers. Budget an extra $150-200/person/day for lift passes and hire.
