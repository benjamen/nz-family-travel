Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1

Wellington City

📍 Wellington 💰 ~$200–350
  • Te Papa Museum (3–4 hours)
  • Wellington Cable Car return
  • Walk back through Botanical Garden
  • Waterfront dinner
Tips:
  • Book Te Papa night tours in advance
  • The cable car runs until 10pm — go at dusk for harbour views
🏕️ Stay: Wellington CBD hotel or TOP 10 Holiday Park Find accommodation →
Day 2

Zealandia + Departure

📍 Wellington 💰 ~$250–400
  • Zealandia morning visit (book ahead)
  • Midday Interislander Ferry (3h through Marlborough Sounds)
  • Arrive Picton — explore the waterfront
Tips:
  • Book the ferry well ahead — family compartments book out fast
  • Sit outside on the ferry for the Marlborough Sounds scenery
  • Kids love spotting dolphins from the deck
🏕️ Stay: Picton or Blenheim Find accommodation →
Day 3

Drive to Nelson

📍 Marlborough to Nelson 💰 ~$150–250
  • Stop at Pelorus Bridge Scenic Reserve (swimming hole, beautiful bush)
  • Nelson city arrival
  • Tahunanui Beach afternoon
  • Nelson Saturday Market if weekend
Tips:
  • Pelorus Bridge swimming hole is one of NZ's best-kept secrets — don't skip it
  • Tahunanui Beach has a great playground right on the sand
🏕️ Stay: Nelson city or Tahunanui Beach Holiday Park Find accommodation →
Day 4

Abel Tasman Day

📍 Kaiteriteri / Abel Tasman 💰 ~$300–450
  • Water taxi from Kaiteriteri to Anchorage Bay
  • Beach day — swimming, snorkelling, exploring
  • Water taxi return
  • Mapua Wharf dinner (30 min from Nelson)
Tips:
  • Book water taxi 2+ weeks ahead in peak season
  • Bring snorkelling gear — the water is clear enough to see fish
  • Mapua Wharf has the best food in the region — fish and chips, craft beer, gelato
🏕️ Stay: Nelson or Kaiteriteri Find accommodation →
Day 5

Departure Day

📍 Nelson 💰 ~$50–100
  • World of WearableArt museum (allow 2h)
  • Or: relaxed morning, café in Nelson
  • Fly home from Nelson Airport or continue to Christchurch via SH6 (4h 30m)
Tips:
  • WoW Museum is excellent and often overlooked — not just fashion
  • Nelson to Christchurch via SH6 through Murchison is a beautiful drive if extending your trip

FAQ

Is the Interislander Ferry good for kids?

Kids love it. Three hours through the Marlborough Sounds, with a café, play areas, and the chance to spot dolphins and seals from the deck. One of the highlights of the trip.

Can you do this in reverse (Nelson to Wellington)?

Yes — just as good. Some families fly into Nelson and take the ferry back to Wellington at the end of the trip.
