Milford Sound Cruise Review

The Milford Sound Nature Cruise is a must-do experience for any family visiting Fiordland, NZ. With its breathtaking scenery and abundant wildlife, it's an adventure your kids will never forget. But what's it really like with kids, and is it value for money?

The Cruise Experience

The Milford Sound Nature Cruise is an unforgettable experience for the whole family. The sheer 1,500-metre peaks rising straight from the water, the stunning Stirling and Lady Bowen Falls, and the regular appearances of dolphins surfing the bow wave of your boat will leave you and your kids in awe.

Rain actually adds to the experience, creating hundreds of extra waterfalls that cascade down the mountains. The 2-hour cruise is the perfect length for kids, keeping them engaged and entertained throughout.

The minimum age for the cruise is 0+, making it accessible to families with babies and toddlers. However, be prepared for potential seasickness, especially if your child is prone to it.

  • Bring warm clothing, as it can get chilly on the water
  • Don't forget your camera to capture the stunning scenery
  • Consider bringing snacks and water to keep your kids satisfied

Value for Money

The cost of the Milford Sound Nature Cruise is $95 per adult and $30 per child. While it may seem steep, the experience is truly priceless. The knowledgeable and friendly crew, the stunning scenery, and the abundance of wildlife make it an experience that's hard to put a price on.

However, if you're on a tight budget, consider visiting during the off-season (April to September) when prices may be slightly lower.

Insider Tips and Booking

To make the most of your experience, book your cruise in advance to avoid sold-out situations, especially during peak season (October to March). You can book online or through a local tour operator.

Consider booking a morning cruise to avoid the crowds and make the most of the calm morning waters.

  • Book online in advance to secure your spot
  • Choose a morning cruise for a more peaceful experience
  • Check the weather forecast before your cruise to prepare for any conditions

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Milford Sound is during the summer months (December to February) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also the peak tourist season, and the cruises can get crowded.

If you prefer a more peaceful experience, consider visiting during the shoulder season (April to May or September to November) when the weather is still mild and the crowds are smaller.

Downsides for Families

While the Milford Sound Nature Cruise is an incredible experience for families, there are a few downsides to consider. The cruise can be quite crowded, especially during peak season, and the boat may not be suitable for children with severe mobility issues.

Additionally, the drive to Milford Sound from Te Anau or Queenstown can be long (around 4-5 hours), so be prepared for a big day out with the kids.
