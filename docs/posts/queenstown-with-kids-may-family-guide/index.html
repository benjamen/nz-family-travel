Queenstown May

May is a fantastic time to visit Queenstown with kids, with mild autumn weather and a range of exciting activities to enjoy. The town is less crowded than in peak summer months, making it easier to book your favourite attractions. With a budget of $350-600 per day for a family of four, you can have a wonderful time exploring all that Queenstown has to offer.

Weather in May

May is a great time to visit Queenstown if you prefer milder weather. Daytime temperatures range from 10-18°C, while nights can be chilly, around 2-6°C. It's a good idea to pack layers for your family, as well as waterproof gear in case of rain.

With the autumn foliage in full swing, Queenstown is particularly beautiful in May, making it an ideal time for scenic walks and photography.

  • Average high: 14°C
  • Average low: 4°C
  • Rainfall: 70mm

What's On in May

May is a great time to experience Queenstown's events and festivals. The Queenstown Winter Festival usually takes place in June, but some winter activities and sales start in late May.

The Remarkables Market is still running every Saturday, offering a great atmosphere, fresh food, and local crafts that kids love.

  • Remarkables Market: every Saturday
  • Queenstown Winter Festival preparation

Best Activities in May

May is an excellent time to enjoy Queenstown's outdoor activities, such as the Skyline Gondola and Luge, Ziptrek Ecotours, and the Kelvin Heights Peninsula loop walk.

The Queenstown Ice Arena is also a great place to visit, with public skating sessions available for all ages.

  • Skyline Gondola and Luge: $40-$60 per person
  • Ziptrek Ecotours: $150-$200 per person
  • Queenstown Ice Arena: $20-$30 per person

Crowd Levels and Booking Ahead

May is considered part of the shoulder season in Queenstown, which means smaller crowds and shorter queues for popular attractions.

However, it's still a good idea to book ahead for activities like the Skyline Gondola and Luge, Ziptrek Ecotours, and Kjet/Dart River Jet to avoid disappointment.

  • Book Skyline Gondola and Luge at least 24 hours in advance
  • Book Ziptrek Ecotours at least 48 hours in advance

Practical Tips for Families

When visiting Queenstown with kids in May, be sure to pack warm clothing, including hats, gloves, and scarves.

Take advantage of the town's free activities, such as the Queenstown Gardens and frisbee golf, and the Lake Wakatipu foreshore walk.

  • Pack warm clothing for your family
  • Take advantage of free activities
  • Book a hotel or apartment with a kitchen to save on food costs
  • Consider purchasing a Queenstown Card for discounts on attractions
  • Check the weather forecast before heading out each day
