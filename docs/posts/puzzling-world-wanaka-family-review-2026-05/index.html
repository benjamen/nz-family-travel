Puzzling World Review

Puzzling World in Wanaka is a must-visit attraction for families, offering a unique and entertaining experience for both kids and adults. With its 3D illusion rooms, holographic gallery, and outdoor maze, there's something for everyone to enjoy. In this review, we'll dive into what it's like to visit Puzzling World with kids, its age suitability, and tips on how to make the most of your visit.

What to Expect

Puzzling World is an excellent choice for families, with its interactive and mind-bending exhibits that cater to a wide range of ages. The 3D illusion rooms are genuinely mind-bending, and the holographic gallery is eerie and fun. The outdoor maze, which takes around 30-60 minutes to complete, is a great way to get some fresh air and exercise.

The attraction is suitable for kids aged 4 and above, and adults will enjoy it just as much as the kids. With a duration of 1.5-2.5 hours, it's an ideal activity for a morning or afternoon outing.

  • 3D illusion rooms
  • Holographic gallery
  • Outdoor maze

Value for Money

Puzzling World is priced at $24 per adult and $18 per child, which is reasonable considering the unique experience it offers. The attraction is partially rain-safe, making it a great option for rainy days.

While it may not be the cheapest activity in Wanaka, it's definitely worth the cost. The quality of the exhibits and the overall experience make it a great value for money.

Insider Tips and Booking

To make the most of your visit, it's best to book your tickets in advance, especially during peak season. You can book online through the Puzzling World website or at the door.

Consider visiting during the morning or late afternoon to avoid the crowds. Additionally, wear comfortable shoes, as you'll be doing a fair amount of walking, especially in the outdoor maze.

  • Book tickets in advance
  • Visit during off-peak hours
  • Wear comfortable shoes

Downsides for Families

While Puzzling World is an excellent attraction for families, there are a few downsides to consider. The outdoor maze can be challenging for younger kids, and some of the exhibits may be too intense for sensitive children.

Additionally, the attraction can get crowded during peak season, which may detract from the overall experience. However, these downsides are minor, and Puzzling World remains a must-visit attraction for families in Wanaka.
