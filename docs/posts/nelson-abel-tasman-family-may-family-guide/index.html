Nelson & Abel Tasman in May

May is a great time to visit Nelson and Abel Tasman with kids, with mild autumn weather, fewer crowds, and lower prices. The region's beautiful beaches, clear waters, and native bush are perfect for outdoor activities like hiking, kayaking, and swimming. With a budget of $220-400/day for a family of 4, you can enjoy a range of activities and experiences.

Weather in May

May is autumn in Nelson and Abel Tasman, with mild temperatures ranging from 10-18°C. The weather is usually calm and sunny, with an average of 4 hours of sunshine per day. It's a great time to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, kayaking, and swimming.

However, the weather can be unpredictable, so it's essential to pack layers and waterproof gear. The region can experience some rainfall in May, but it's usually short-lived and followed by sunshine.

  • Average temperature: 10-18°C
  • Average sunshine: 4 hours/day
  • Rainfall: 80-100 mm

What's On in May

May is a great time to visit Nelson and Abel Tasman, with a range of events and activities to enjoy. The Nelson Saturday Market is a must-visit, with local produce, crafts, and live music.

The region's national parks and beaches are also less crowded in May, making it a great time to explore the outdoors. You can hike the Abel Tasman coastal track, kayak to secluded beaches, or simply relax on the beach.

  • Nelson Saturday Market
  • Abel Tasman coastal track hike
  • Kayaking and swimming

Best Activities in May

May is a great time to enjoy outdoor activities in Nelson and Abel Tasman. The Abel Tasman Water Taxi and Beach Day is a must-do, with prices starting from $40-$60 per person for a half-day tour.

Sea kayaking is another popular activity, with prices starting from $80-$120 per person for a full-day tour. You can also visit the Founders Heritage Park in Nelson, with prices starting from $10-$20 per person.

  • Abel Tasman Water Taxi and Beach Day: $40-$60 per person
  • Sea kayaking: $80-$120 per person
  • Founders Heritage Park: $10-$20 per person

Practical Tips for Families

May is a great time to visit Nelson and Abel Tasman with kids, but it's essential to plan ahead. Book your accommodation and activities in advance, especially during school holidays.

Here are some practical tips for families visiting in May:

  • Book accommodation and activities in advance
  • Pack layers and waterproof gear
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Check the weather forecast before heading out
  • Consider visiting the region's free beaches, such as Tahunanui Beach and Kaiteriteri Beach

Crowd Levels and Booking Ahead

May is considered the shoulder season in Nelson and Abel Tasman, with fewer crowds than the peak summer months. However, it's still essential to book your accommodation and activities in advance, especially during school holidays.

Some popular activities, such as the Abel Tasman Water Taxi and sea kayaking, can book out quickly, so it's essential to plan ahead. You can book these activities online or through a local tour operator.

  • Book accommodation and activities in advance
  • Check the availability of popular activities
  • Consider visiting the region's less crowded beaches and attractions
