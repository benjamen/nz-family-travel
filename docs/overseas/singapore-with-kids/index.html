Is Singapore Good for Families?

Singapore is not a cheap destination, but for families it is extraordinary value in experience-per-dollar terms. Universal Studios Singapore, Gardens by the Bay (the free Supertree night show alone is worth stopping for), Sentosa Island, and the world's best food courts (hawker centres) are all here in a spotlessly clean, fully English-speaking, highly efficient city-state. Most NZ families visit Singapore as a 3-5 day stopover en route to or from Bali, Thailand, or Vietnam — and many find Singapore itself the highlight of the trip.

Our verdict

Exceptional for 3-5 day family stopovers. Safe, English-speaking, efficient, and packed with world-class family experiences. Expensive but delivers extremely high quality for the money.

Budget (family of 4)

NZ$500-1,000/day all-in — accommodation, food, activities and local transport.

Best Places in Singapore for Families

NZ$200-300/person for Universal Studios. Budget NZ$600-800 for family of 4 for a full Sentosa day.

Sentosa Island — Universal Studios and Beaches

Singapore's island playground — theme park, beaches, and cable cars

Sentosa is Singapore's dedicated resort island, 10 minutes from the city centre by cable car or metro. Universal Studios Singapore has excellent rides including Transformers, Jurassic World, and the Minion Land. The island also has beaches (sand is imported, but the water is clean), the S.E.A. Aquarium, and Adventure Cove Waterpark. A full day on Sentosa suits families perfectly.

Universal Studios Singapore — book 1 day Adventure Cove Waterpark — excellent for families Cable car to Sentosa
Free for Supertree Grove. Cloud Forest + Flower Dome: NZ$40/adult, NZ$20/child. Children's Garden: free.

Gardens by the Bay

The Supertree Grove light show is the most spectacular free thing in Singapore

Gardens by the Bay is a futuristic garden on Singapore's Marina Bay waterfront. The Supertrees — 25-50m vertical gardens — put on a free light and music show at 7:45pm and 8:45pm nightly that is genuinely breathtaking. The Cloud Forest dome (two-story waterfall inside a glass dome, NZ$20/adult) is spectacular. The Children's Garden is free and has excellent water play facilities for under-12s.

Supertree Grove light show (free, 7:45pm nightly) Cloud Forest dome Children's Garden water play (free)
NZ$30-50 for a full meal for 4 at any hawker centre.

Hawker Centres — The Real Singapore

The world's best cheap food — $3-6 for world-class dishes

Singapore's hawker centres are UNESCO-listed. A family of 4 can eat extraordinarily well for NZ$25-40 at any hawker centre. Maxwell Food Centre, Lau Pa Sat, and Old Airport Road are the most famous. Hainanese chicken rice, char kway teow, laksa, satay, and kaya toast are all must-tries. Kids who are adventurous with food have a transformative experience. Kids who only eat plain things can always get chicken rice (a genuinely universal crowd-pleaser).

Maxwell Food Centre — Tian Tian Hainanese chicken rice Lau Pa Sat night market (6pm onwards, the satay stalls appear at night) Newton Food Centre (more tourist-friendly, slightly more expensive)

Practical Info — Singapore with NZ Kids

CurrencySingapore Dollar (SGD). NZ$1 = S$0.83 approximately (2026). Eftpos and credit cards accepted almost everywhere. Cash useful for hawker centres.
HealthNo vaccinations required for NZ travellers. Singapore has world-class medical facilities. Dengue fever is present — use mosquito repellent in parks.
SIM cardBuy a Singapore SIM at the airport (NZ$15-25 with 100GB for 30 days, StarHub or Singtel). Instant activation. Excellent coverage.
VisaNZ passport holders get 30 days visa-free on arrival. No pre-application needed.
TransportMRT (metro) is excellent, cheap, and air-conditioned — ideal with kids. Grab (Singapore's Uber) is cheap and reliable. Avoid taxis — more expensive than Grab.
Food safetyExcellent food safety. Hawker centres are government-regulated and safe. Tap water is safe to drink — one of the cleanest water supplies in Asia.

Singapore Family Budget Breakdown

Accommodation per nightNZ$250-500/night for a family room in a good hotel near Orchard Road or Marina Bay. Sentosa Island resorts: NZ$400-800/night.
Food per dayNZ$40-80/day eating at hawker centres and local restaurants. Doubles eating at hotel restaurants or tourist spots.
Activities per dayNZ$200-400/day. Universal Studios NZ$180-250/adult. Gardens by the Bay Cloud Forest NZ$40/adult. Night Safari NZ$60/adult.
Local transport per dayNZ$20-40/day for MRT and Grab. Airport taxi or Grab to city: NZ$25-40.

Singapore Airlines flies Auckland-Singapore direct and is consistently rated the world's best airline — the service for families is exceptional. Air NZ also flies direct. Singapore makes an ideal 3-5 day stopover when travelling to Southeast Asia — often the same price as flying direct to Bangkok or Bali with a Singapore stopover added. Book accommodation in Orchard Road or Marina Bay Sands area for the best access to all major attractions.
