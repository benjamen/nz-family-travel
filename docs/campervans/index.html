Should You Hire a Campervan for Your NZ Family Trip?

✓ Go campervan if…

  • Your trip is 7+ days
  • You're doing a road trip (not staying in one city)
  • You want to save $600–1500 on accommodation
  • You want the flexibility to stop anywhere
  • You have a family of 4+ who need space

✗ Skip campervan if…

  • Your trip is 5 days or less
  • You're staying in one city (e.g. just Auckland)
  • You need premium accommodation (business travel)
  • You have a baby under 6 months
  • Your budget is very tight (rental car + holiday parks can cost less on short trips)

NZ Campervan FAQ

What is the best campervan for a family of 4 in NZ?

The Britz Explorer (5-berth) or Maui Cascade (4-berth) are the best family options. The Britz has a separate sleeping area for kids which is genuinely useful on longer trips. The Maui is newer and more affordable. Both sleep 4 comfortably.

Do I need a special licence to drive a campervan in NZ?

No. Most campervans in NZ (under 6m) can be driven on a standard car licence. Larger motorhomes over 6m may require a special licence — check with the operator.

Can campervans freedom camp in NZ?

Self-contained campervans (with toilet and water supply) can freedom camp at designated sites. Non-self-contained vans must stay at holiday parks. Check freedomcamping.org.nz for designated sites.

How far in advance should I book a campervan for NZ?

For December–January (peak summer), book 3–6 months ahead. For other months, 6–8 weeks is usually sufficient. One-way bookings (e.g., Auckland to Queenstown) are the most popular and book out fastest.

What is included in a NZ campervan hire?

Most campervans include: bedding, kitchen equipment, GPS, basic camping equipment. Not included: excess insurance reduction ($15–50/day), one-way fees ($300–500 if applicable), additional driver fees. Always read the fine print before booking.
