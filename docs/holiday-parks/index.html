Holiday Park Tips for Families

Book early for school holidays

The best holiday parks — especially at Hot Water Beach, Kaiteriteri, and Queenstown — book out 3–6 months ahead for school holiday periods. Don't leave it to the last month.

TOP 10 membership saves 10%

A TOP 10 annual membership costs around $49 and gives 10% off at all 50+ TOP 10 holiday parks. It pays for itself in 2–3 nights and is worth buying before any family NZ road trip.

Cabins vs powered sites

Most holiday parks offer powered sites (for campervans and tents) and cabins/units. Cabins with bathrooms (called 'tourist units' or 'ensuite cabins') sleep families for $120–$200/night, often much cheaper than motels with better facilities.

Kitchen facilities cut your food budget

Holiday park camp kitchens let you cook your own meals. A family of 4 can eat well for $30–$50/day vs $120+ for three restaurant meals. Most parks have BBQ areas too — stock up at supermarkets.

Arrival time matters

Most holiday parks have check-in from 2pm. Arriving late on a busy summer day can mean smaller or less sheltered sites. Call ahead if running late — they often hold your booking but may re-allocate spots.
