Our Verdict

The Coromandel Peninsula is Auckland's backyard holiday spot — and for good reason. Hot Water Beach (dig your own hot pool in the sand), Cathedral Cove sea arch, Thames goldfields, and a string of gorgeous beaches make it one of the best NZ family destinations. Best in summer.

Best for

Beach holidays, Auckland day trips or overnights, hot spring lovers

Not ideal for

Families wanting theme parks or rainy-day activities — it's very outdoor-focused

Coromandel Family FAQ

When is Hot Water Beach best?

The geothermal seep is only accessible 2 hours either side of low tide. Check hotwatertide.co.nz before you go. Weekday mornings in shoulder season are the least crowded.

Is Cathedral Cove better by boat or on foot?

Boat if you have young kids (avoids the 35-min walk each way). On foot if your kids can manage it — the walk is lovely. The sea arch looks the same either way.

Can you do Coromandel as a day trip from Auckland?

Yes — just about. Hot Water Beach + Cathedral Cove is a very long day (5h driving total). Better to stay 1–2 nights and actually enjoy it.

How far is Hot Water Beach from Auckland?

About 2.5 hours from Auckland via the main state highway, or 3 hours if you take the more scenic Coromandel Town route. It's achievable as a long day trip but overnight is much better — you can dig your hot pool at the optimal low tide time (check tide times before you go — the beach only works within 2 hours either side of low tide). The Coromandel Peninsula is most enjoyable as a 2–3 night trip.

Is Cathedral Cove walkable with kids?

The Cathedral Cove walkway is 3.2km return from the Hahei car park, taking about 1.5 hours return with young kids. The track is well-formed but hilly in places — doable for most kids 5+ with patience. During peak summer (Christmas to late January), cars can't park at the trailhead and a shuttle runs from Hahei. Alternatively, take a water taxi from Hahei (10 minutes) to arrive directly at the cove — far easier with under-5s.

What is there to do in Coromandel besides Hot Water Beach and Cathedral Cove?

Quite a lot: the Coromandel Walkway (if your kids are older, 7km point-to-point), Driving Creek Railway (a narrow-gauge scenic railway — excellent for kids of all ages), Thames Goldmine experience, kayaking from Hahei or Whangamata, and Whangapoua Beach (one of the most beautiful and least-visited beaches on the peninsula). The whole peninsula has excellent fish and chips at local takeaways — a NZ coastal tradition.

Hidden Gems — Coromandel

Stuff the guidebooks miss — local knowledge from NZ families who've done it properly.

  • 🔍 Hot Water Beach at dawn — arrive 90 mins before low tide on a weekday morning. You'll have it almost to yourself vs the chaotic peak-time crowds
  • 🔍 Opito Bay — one of the most stunning beaches on the peninsula, hard to find, rarely crowded. Locals go here instead of Hahei
  • 🔍 Te Whanganui-A-Hei Marine Reserve snorkelling near Cathedral Cove — some of the best snorkelling in NZ, completely free
  • 🔍 Coromandel town (the actual town) — often skipped as people go straight to Hahei. The Coromandel Smoking Company smoked fish and chips are legendary ($15 a serve)
  • 🔍 Karangahake Gorge windows walk (Thames side) — goes through old gold mine tunnels with a torch. Free, very cool for kids
  • 🔍 Long Bay near Whitianga — far less visited than Hahei, beautiful swimming, rarely mentioned in guides

Gems Along the Way to Coromandel

Don't miss these on your way through — detours and stops that are worth every minute.

Hot Water Beach

📍 35 min south of Whitianga on SH25

Dig your own thermal spa in the sand at low tide — one of the most unique beaches in the world

💡 Tip: Go 2 hrs either side of low tide. Hire spades from the café ($5). The hot water section is small — arrive early to get a spot.

Cathedral Cove

📍 20 min north of Hahei, near Whitianga

NZ's most photographed natural arch — a stunning sea cavern connecting two beaches

💡 Tip: The 45-min walk is suitable for all ages. Boat or kayak access available from Hahei — the sea cave approach is spectacular.

Miranda Hot Springs

📍 1 hr south of Thames via SH25 — en route from Auckland

Thermal pools by the Firth of Thames with one of NZ's largest outdoor hot pools — great stopover

💡 Tip: Located on the main Auckland–Coromandel highway. Perfect for tired legs at the start or end of a Coromandel trip.

Hamilton Gardens

📍 1.5 hrs south-west via Morrinsville

World-class themed gardens (54 gardens, 58 hectares) — completely free and stunning for all ages

💡 Tip: Combine with Hobbiton at Matamata (45 min from Hamilton) for a great Waikato loop.
