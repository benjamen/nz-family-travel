Our Verdict

Wanaka is what Queenstown was before the crowds arrived. The lake is stunning, the town is relaxed, Puzzling World is a quirky family classic, and Treble Cone offers winter skiing for those with older kids. In summer, it's a beach town with mountains.

Best for

Families who want scenery without Queenstown prices and crowds

Not ideal for

Those who want maximum activity options (fewer than Queenstown)

Wanaka Family FAQ

Is Wanaka better than Queenstown for families?

For young children (under 8) or families wanting a relaxed pace, yes. Wanaka is calmer, cheaper, and the lake is more accessible. For older kids wanting adventure activities, Queenstown has more options.

Is the Crown Range road safe to drive?

Yes, in good weather. It's NZ's highest paved road and spectacular. Avoid in icy conditions (winter/early spring). The alternative via Cromwell is longer but easier.

Is Wanaka better than Queenstown for families?

For younger families (under 10), many parents prefer Wanaka — it's quieter, less commercial, and the lake and town centre are more relaxed. Cardrona ski resort is world-class for families in winter. Puzzling World is a genuine Wanaka institution. The lake is spectacular for swimming and kayaking in summer. Queenstown has more activities and more restaurant variety, but Wanaka has more soul and is less rushed.

How far is Wanaka from Queenstown?

About 1 hour via the direct Crown Range Road (the highest sealed road in NZ — spectacular views but steep and winding, not suitable for campervans or in icy conditions). Via the main highway through Cromwell it's about 75 minutes. The Crown Range is worth doing in good conditions — the views from the top are extraordinary. Check conditions in winter before attempting it.

What is the best time to visit Wanaka with kids?

Summer (Dec–Feb) for lake swimming, hiking, and outdoor activities — the town is busy but manageable. Autumn (March–May) for stunning golden colours around the lake, fewer crowds, and great hiking conditions. Winter (June–Sept) for Cardrona skiing — Wanaka is much quieter than Queenstown for ski families. Spring (Oct–Nov) is underrated — warming up, lake accessible, uncrowded. Avoid peak January if you hate queues.

Hidden Gems — Wanaka

Stuff the guidebooks miss — local knowledge from NZ families who've done it properly.

  • 🔍 Diamond Lake and Rocky Mountain track — a 3-hour loop starting 20 min from Wanaka. Stunning views of Lake Wanaka and the Matukituki Valley. Suitable for kids 7+
  • 🔍 Wanaka's famous lone willow tree is best at sunset or early morning — avoid midday when it's packed with photographers
  • 🔍 Mount Iron track (45 min return from near town) — a volcanic dome with 360-degree views. Kids 6+ can handle it
  • 🔍 Glendhu Bay — 12km from Wanaka, a DOC campsite with one of the best lake swimming spots in the South Island. $10/person/night to stay overnight
  • 🔍 Wanaka Beerworks has a child-friendly tap room with non-alcoholic options — a local hangout that's actually relaxed for families
  • 🔍 Stuart Landsborough's Puzzling World: buy the combined illusion room + maze ticket ($18 adult, $13 child). The maze alone takes 45+ minutes — seriously tricky
  • 🔍 Cardona ski field road (don't ski) — drive up the Cardona Valley road in summer for jaw-dropping scenery and alpine meadow walks

Gems Along the Way to Wanaka

Don't miss these on your way through — detours and stops that are worth every minute.

Puzzling World

📍 10 min east of Wanaka on SH84

Optical illusions, the famous leaning tower, and NZ's best illusion rooms — kids go nuts for it

💡 Tip: Allow 2 hrs. The Hologram Hall and Room of Following Faces are the highlights. Book online for a small discount.

Arrowtown

📍 40 min south via Crown Range Road

Gold-rush history, Arrow River gold panning, and NZ's best autumn colours

💡 Tip: Crown Range Road (SH89) is the scenic route — highest sealed road in NZ, stunning views. Allow extra time.

Cromwell Old Town

📍 30 min south on SH6

Historic goldfields township, Central Otago stone fruit, and the Clutha River — great family lunch stop

💡 Tip: The historic precinct is free to walk. Grab stone fruit at the roadside stalls December–March.

Cardrona Alpine Resort

📍 30 min south of Wanaka via Cardrona Valley Road

Family-friendly ski field with excellent beginners' terrain and a great snow tubing park for young kids

💡 Tip: Better for families with young kids than Treble Cone. Cardrona kids' learning area is excellent.
