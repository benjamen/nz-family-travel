Our Verdict

Northland is where New Zealand begins — Cape Reinga at the very tip, Ninety Mile Beach stretching impossibly far, the Bay of Islands (its own destination), and ancient kauri trees that were old when Rome fell. It is warm, uncrowded outside peak season, and full of both Maori history and natural spectacle.

Best for

Families who love beach walks, history, and genuinely off-the-beaten-track NZ

Not ideal for

Families who need packed activity schedules — Northland rewards slow travel

3-Day Northland Family Itinerary

A structured day-by-day plan — adjust to your pace.

1

Auckland to Northland — Kauri Forest and Hokianga

~$100-180
  • Drive from Auckland north (2.5h to Waipoua)
  • Tane Mahuta walkway — see the largest living kauri tree in NZ (free, 10-min walk)
  • Hokianga Harbour — ferry to Omapere sand dunes
  • Night at Rawene or Opononi
2

Far North — Cape Reinga and Ninety Mile Beach

~$120-250
  • Drive north to Kaitaia (1h 30m from Hokianga)
  • Optional: join a Cape Reinga day tour (includes Ninety Mile Beach sandboarding, $85-120/person)
  • Cape Reinga lighthouse walk — 30 minutes return, the spiritual departure point of Maori souls
  • Te Paki Stream — body boards down the stream outlet to the beach (bring your own or hire)
3

Doubtless Bay and Karikari Peninsula

~$50-100
  • Matai Bay — double beach, protected water, perfect for young swimmers
  • Mangonui fish and chip shop — one of NZ's most famous fish and chip shops, eat on the wharf
  • Drive south back to Auckland or continue to Bay of Islands

Northland Family FAQ

Is Cape Reinga worth the drive with kids?

Yes, genuinely — but it is a long day from Paihia (4h return driving) or an even longer day from Auckland. The tip of NZ where two oceans meet is a genuinely moving experience, and the Ninety Mile Beach sandboarding on the way back is a family highlight. Join an organised day tour rather than self-driving — the tour takes the beach route which is only accessible to experienced 4WD drivers.

What is the best base for a Northland family holiday?

Paihia (Bay of Islands) works for the southern Northland attractions. For the Far North (Cape Reinga, Doubtless Bay), base at Kaitaia or Mangonui. For Hokianga and Waipoua Forest, base at Opononi. A 4-5 day Northland loop starting and ending in Auckland covers all the main areas.

Hidden Gems — Northland

Stuff the guidebooks miss — local knowledge from NZ families who've done it properly.

  • 🔍 Matai Bay (Karikari Peninsula) — two beaches facing different directions, protected water perfect for kids, barely signposted
  • 🔍 Tokerau Beach on Karikari Peninsula — one of NZ's finest beaches, almost deserted outside New Year
  • 🔍 Wenderholm Regional Park — first regional park south of Whangarei, beautiful beach and pohutukawa trees
  • 🔍 Pukenui wharf — a humble fishing wharf where locals catch snapper right off the dock, kids can watch or try their luck
  • 🔍 Matauri Bay — stunning beach where the Rainbow Warrior wreck sits offshore (visible by snorkel)
  • 🔍 Waipu Caves — glowworm cave you can self-explore with a torch, $5 entry, virtually unknown
