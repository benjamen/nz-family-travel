Our Verdict

Taupo sits at the centre of the North Island and makes the perfect stopping point on any road trip. The Huka Falls are spectacular and free, Lake Taupo is huge and swimmable, and the town has enough to keep a family busy for 2–3 days without blowing the budget.

Best for

All ages, especially great for a relaxed lake holiday

Taupo Family FAQ

Can you swim in Lake Taupo?

Yes — Acacia Bay and Wharewaka Point are the best family swimming spots. Clean, clear water. Cold outside of December–February.

Is the Spa Thermal Park really free?

Yes. It's a natural hot spring on the Waikato River bank, accessed via a walking track. No facilities, no fee. Bring a towel.

How far is Taupo from Rotorua?

About 1 hour by car. Most families combine both into one trip.

Is Huka Falls free to visit?

Yes — Huka Falls is completely free and one of the most spectacular sights in New Zealand. The car park is free, the walkway is flat and pram-friendly (300m to the viewing platform), and there are toilets on-site. It's the most-visited natural attraction in NZ for good reason — the volume and colour of the water is genuinely jaw-dropping.

Can kids swim in Lake Taupo?

Yes — Lake Taupo is one of the best freshwater swimming lakes in NZ. The water is clean and clear, though cold (16–20°C in summer, much colder in winter). Acacia Bay on the western shore is sheltered and popular with families. The main town beach (Two Mile Bay) also has good swimming with a playground nearby. The lake is geothermally warmed very slightly but still cool.

Is Taupo worth a full day or just a stop?

Taupo easily fills 2–3 days for families. Day 1: Huka Falls (free), Craters of the Moon walk, Taupo lakefront and beach. Day 2: Huka Jet boat, AC Baths hot pools, evening at the i-SITE foreshore. Day 3: Great Lake Trail cycling, Wairakei Thermal Valley, Aratiatia Rapids (check dam release times). It's also a natural halfway stop on the Rotorua–Wellington drive.

Hidden Gems — Taupo

Stuff the guidebooks miss — local knowledge from NZ families who've done it properly.

  • 🔍 Taupo Bungy spectator area is free — kids love watching the bungee jumpers launch out over the Waikato River
  • 🔍 Huka Prawn Park has a FREE prawn feeding area — you only pay if you want to fish
  • 🔍 Acacia Bay is Taupo's best-kept secret — calm swimming, sandy shore, no tourists, 10 min from town centre
  • 🔍 Walk the Huka Falls from upstream — start at Wairakei Park, walk 1km to the falls. Completely free, incredible turquoise water views
  • 🔍 Craters of the Moon (geothermal walk): adults $10, children free. Less crowded than Te Puia and still spectacular
  • 🔍 Taupo Night Market runs Thursday evenings in summer — $5 hangi ($20 for family of 4), live music, craft stalls
  • 🔍 Wairakei Terraces has a FREE hot stream walking path along the edge of the property — steam, pools, no entry fee

Gems Along the Way to Taupo

Don't miss these on your way through — detours and stops that are worth every minute.

Huka Falls

📍 10 min north of Taupo town

The Waikato River squeezes through a narrow gorge and explodes — NZ's most-visited natural attraction, free

💡 Tip: Park at the Huka Falls car park. Short walk (5 min). Jet boat tours run from here too — thrilling for kids 6+.

Tongariro Alpine Crossing

📍 2 hrs south via Ohakune

NZ's best day walk — volcanic craters, emerald lakes, and Mount Doom (Mt Ngauruhoe). Teens will love it.

💡 Tip: Too hard for under-10s. Shuttle from Ohakune or Whakapapa. Start early — 7am for the full crossing.

Orakei Korako Geothermal Park

📍 45 min north of Taupo via Wairakei

The Hidden Valley — active geysers, silica terraces, and a cave with geothermal pool. Less touristy than Rotorua.

💡 Tip: Ferry crossing included in entry. Allow 90 mins. Excellent rainy-day option.

Waitomo Caves

📍 1.5 hrs north-west via Hamilton

Glowworm caves on the classic Golden Triangle loop — Auckland → Waitomo → Rotorua → Taupo

💡 Tip: Do this as part of the drive from Auckland rather than a separate day trip from Taupo.
