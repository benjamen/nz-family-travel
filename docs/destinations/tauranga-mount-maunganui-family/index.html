Our Verdict

Tauranga and Mount Maunganui ('the Mount') is where New Zealand families have been going for summer holidays for generations. Mount Beach is NZ's best family beach — gentle surf, lifeguards, warm water, and excellent town amenities. It is unpretentious, warm, and has everything a family needs within walking distance.

Best for

Beach holidays, families with kids who want to surf or bodyboard, relaxed summer holidays

Not ideal for

Families who need cultural or activity-heavy itineraries — it is primarily a beach destination

Tauranga / Mount Maunganui Family FAQ

Is Mount Maunganui beach safe for young children?

Mount Beach has lifeguards from December to February and is generally one of NZ's safer surf beaches for families. Pilot Bay on the harbour side of the Mount is completely sheltered and perfect for toddlers. Young children who cannot swim confidently should stick to Pilot Bay or the shallow shore break at Mount Beach between the flags.

How far is Tauranga from Rotorua?

Just under 1 hour (62km). This makes Tauranga an excellent beach base with easy day-trip access to Rotorua's geothermal parks and farm shows. A 5-day trip could combine 3 nights at the Mount (beach focus) with 2 nights in Rotorua.

Is Mount Maunganui beach safe for young swimmers?

Yes — the main Mount Maunganui beach (Main Beach) is one of the safest patrolled beaches in the Bay of Plenty, with Surf Life Saving patrols operating in summer. The surf is generally moderate and the beach shelves gradually. Pilot Bay (the harbour side of the Mount) is calmer still — ideal for toddlers and non-swimmers. The Mount Hot Pools are also family-friendly with a shallow pool area for younger children.

Can you climb Mount Maunganui with kids?

Yes — the Mauao base track (flat, 3.4km around the base) is suitable for all ages and prams. The summit walk (45 min return, steep in parts) is achievable for kids 6+ who are reasonably fit. Views from the top are excellent — the Bay of Plenty coastline is spectacular on a clear day. Start early in summer to avoid the heat. The base track through the reserve passes native bush with tūī and kererū (wood pigeons).

What is the drive time from Auckland to Tauranga?

About 2.5–3 hours from Auckland via the Kaimais (SH2). The main pass road is steep and winding in places — take it slowly with young passengers. Via Hamilton (SH1 then SH29) is about the same time and flatter. Tauranga is best combined with a Coromandel Peninsula trip — you can do a loop: Auckland → Coromandel → Whitianga → Tauranga → Rotorua → Auckland in a 5–7 day road trip.

Hidden Gems — Tauranga / Mount Maunganui

Stuff the guidebooks miss — local knowledge from NZ families who've done it properly.

  • 🔍 Pilot Bay on the harbour side of the Mount — completely sheltered from surf, perfect for toddlers learning to swim, almost unknown to tourists
  • 🔍 Omanu Beach (north of Mount Beach) — same sand, slightly less crowded, popular with local families
  • 🔍 The Strand in Tauranga — the city's main waterfront, good for an evening walk with excellent cafes
  • 🔍 McLaren Falls Park — 20 mins south of Tauranga, beautiful waterfall and lake, free, rarely visited by tourists
  • 🔍 Waihi Beach — 40 mins north, beautiful and quieter than the Mount with the same sand quality
  • 🔍 Katikati Bird Gardens — small but excellent, rarely on tourist itineraries

Gems Along the Way to Tauranga / Mount Maunganui

Don't miss these on your way through — detours and stops that are worth every minute.

Hobbiton Movie Set

📍 1 hr south-west via Matamata

The most magical film set in NZ — you have to see the Shire at least once

💡 Tip: 45 min each way from Tauranga. Book the 10am tour to beat the crowds. Under-9s free.

McLaren Falls Park

📍 30 min south of Tauranga via SH29

Stunning waterfalls in a DOC park with swimming holes, BBQ areas, and glowworm dell at night

💡 Tip: Free to visit. The glowworm dell is 500m from the car park — go after dark with a torch. Swimming is beautiful December–March.

Waihi Beach & Gold Mine

📍 45 min north of Mount Maunganui

Long surf beach plus the Waihi Gold Mine Experience — gold mining history meets gorgeous swimming

💡 Tip: Waihi Beach is less crowded than Mount Maunganui. The Gold Mine Experience ($30/adult) is genuinely impressive — NZ's largest open-cast mine.

Coromandel Peninsula

📍 1.5 hrs north to Whitianga — Hot Water Beach and Cathedral Cove

Hot Water Beach thermal spa, Cathedral Cove sea arch, and the gorgeous Coromandel coastline

💡 Tip: A natural loop: Tauranga → Waihi → Whitianga → Hot Water Beach → Thames → Auckland (or back south).
