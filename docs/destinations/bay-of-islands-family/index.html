Our Verdict

The Bay of Islands is NZ's original holiday destination and still one of the best. Paihia is the main family base — from there you can swim with dolphins, sail to deserted islands, visit the Waitangi Treaty Grounds, and enjoy some of the warmest water in the country.

Best for

Beach-loving families, water activities, NZ history

Not ideal for

Those wanting to avoid the 3.5-hour drive from Auckland

Bay of Islands Family FAQ

Can you actually swim with dolphins at the Bay of Islands?

Yes, but it depends on the dolphins cooperating. Most operators run swim/watch tours. Guaranteed sightings but not guaranteed in-water encounters. Book with Explore NZ or Fullers.

How far is Bay of Islands from Auckland?

About 3.5 hours by car via SH1. Most families drive up — no direct train. Flights take 40 mins to Kerikeri.

Is Russell worth visiting?

Yes — take the 10-minute ferry from Paihia ($15 return). Russell is NZ's first European settlement, quiet and historic. Great for lunch.

Hidden Gems — Bay of Islands

Stuff the guidebooks miss — local knowledge from NZ families who've done it properly.

  • 🔍 Haruru Falls — a horseshoe waterfall 10 min from Paihia. Free, easy 20-min walk from the car park, completely off most tourist itineraries
  • 🔍 Waitangi Mangrove boardwalk — free 1km boardwalk through the mangroves behind the Treaty Grounds. Kids love spotting fish and mud crabs
  • 🔍 Russell is 15 min by ferry from Paihia ($8 return) — quieter, historic, better cafes, gorgeous beaches (Long Beach is a 10-min walk from town)
  • 🔍 Urupukapuka Island day trips — DOC-run island with ancient Maori pa sites and stunning beaches. Ferry from Paihia $45 adult, $23 child return
  • 🔍 Poor Knights Islands glass-bottom boat: you don't need to scuba dive — the glass-bottom tours show the underwater world for all ages from Tutukaka
  • 🔍 Paihia Night Market (summer Fridays) — fresh seafood, live music, $5–10 snacks, very family-friendly
  • 🔍 Cape Brett lighthouse walk (experienced hikers only) — 15km return, takes all day, rewards with dramatic coastal views and a possible guided hut overnight
