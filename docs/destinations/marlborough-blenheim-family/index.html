Our Verdict

Marlborough is the South Island's sunniest region and the gateway between the ferry and the rest of the South Island. Families come for the Marlborough Sounds' sheltered swimming bays, wildlife cruises, the Queen Charlotte Track, and surprisingly good food. Blenheim is a great overnight stop, and the Sounds offer some of the best kayaking for kids anywhere in NZ.

Marlborough & Blenheim Family FAQ

Is Marlborough good for families with young kids?

Yes, especially for water-confident kids 5+. The sheltered Sounds bays are perfect for swimming, kayaking, and boat trips. Momorangi Bay is ideal for toddlers — calm, warm, shallow water.

Is the Queen Charlotte Track suitable for families?

Short sections are. The Ship Cove to Resolution Bay section (2.5hr, easy) is the most popular with families. You take a water taxi out and walk back or take a water taxi both ways. Book with Cougar Line or Beachcomber Cruises in Picton.

Where is the best swimming in Marlborough?

Pelorus Bridge for river swimming (crystal clear, stunning bush setting), Momorangi Bay for sheltered Sounds sea swimming. Both are safe for children and free.

Hidden Gems — Marlborough & Blenheim

Stuff the guidebooks miss — local knowledge from NZ families who've done it properly.

  • 🔍 Pelorus Bridge Scenic Reserve — crystal clear river swimming 45min from Blenheim, unforgettable in summer
  • 🔍 Momorangi Bay — sheltered Sounds bay, calm warm water, ideal for small kids
  • 🔍 Hopewell Lodge water taxi — only way in, felt like a secret world
  • 🔍 Rarangi Beach — wild windy beach near Blenheim, seals sometimes visible
  • 🔍 Omaka Aviation Heritage Centre — WW1 and WW2 planes with Peter Jackson's theatrical displays
